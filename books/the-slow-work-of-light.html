The Slow Work of Light

Sky García
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Sky García has lived in four countries, spoken in four languages, and spent seventy years learning how to pay attention. This collection is the record of that education — not a memoir, not a reckoning, but something quieter and more demanding: a sustained practice of noticing. The haiku gathered here do not announce their travels or their losses. They arrive, instead, at the threshold of small things — a gas ring catching, a word dissolving in the air, light bending into brown water — and ask the reader to stand there too, long enough for something to open. García works in a tradition that prizes compression, but these poems carry weight the way old wood does: not from ornament but from use. The body that moves through these pages has accumulated four countries' worth of texture — their smells, their grammars, their particular qualities of morning — and has learned, slowly, that accumulation is not the same as loss. What emerges across these eight sections is not a portrait of a life survived but of a life still being lived, attentively, with the specific gratitude of someone who has finally stopped mistaking busyness for presence. The title is both method and subject. Light does its work without urgency, without announcement, finding the crack above the shoji panel, striping the Bangkok wall and the Osaka wall with the same gold, indifferent to the decades between. García has learned to work the same way — slowly, consistently, without demanding that the moment yield more than it is. These poems are the result: not wisdom literature, not elegy, but the daily evidence of a mind still enrolled in the world.

worn through, the binding—

is just a window

a market in Namba:

galangal root, kaffir lime —

the stomach remembers

she wore that scarf through

November, December, snow —

I wonder if she still does

I stood at the rail

of Nakanoshima bridge —

the leaf went without me

red pen, then blue pen,

then my thumbnail — the line

has been handled enough
