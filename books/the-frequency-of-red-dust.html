The Frequency of Red Dust

Harper Sato
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A haiku is a small room with no furniture. You bring yourself in, and the room shows you what you are carrying. Harper Sato has built two hundred and fifty such rooms across three cities, three names, and the long life of a self in translation — and what you will find inside them is not confession exactly, but something more precise: the sensation of a life at the exact moment it becomes aware of itself. Red dust. A drooping mic flag. The cold run of the Rhône. The workshop going quiet. These are not symbols. They are the actual weight of actual things, and Sato trusts them the way a jazz musician trusts a single note held just past its welcome, into the silence that gives it meaning.

red dust climbs the wheel—

the lorry already gone,

my mouth still tasting

the teacher pauses—

my surname in her mouth like

a stone she must place

he shook my hand twice

which means something I will keep

from the final draft

the workshop window

shows a parking lot, one oak—

I watch the oak win

my tea goes cold while

I reread what I have signed —

outside, a tram bell

stand at the tip, watch

both rivers slide past — neither

one asks where I'm from
