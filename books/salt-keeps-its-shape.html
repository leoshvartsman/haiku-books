the blue tile sweats—

a crack runs from saint to sea

my thumb finds the gap

summer splits the glaze

what the painter meant to keep

leaks into the light

cracked azulejo—

the grout remembers winter

the face forgets rain

a fig falls, not picked

it opens on the cobblestone

purple, then the ants

the fig tree in heat

drops one leaf on the dry stone

the stone does not cool

frost on the oval—

below, the Atlantic hides

its unreadable floor

the workshop table

wobbles on one short leg—

we hold it level

subway at rush hour,

a man recites something soft,

lips moving, no sound

October maples

in the Ramble, one by one

releasing the red

a window at the

corridor end frames the sea

I pass it running

pohutukawa

petals on the sill — no one

put them there at all

the stethoscope loop

leaves a red crease on my neck

I wear it to lunch

rain on kauri bark

sounds different from rain on tiles—

I had to learn this

before full light comes

the tui has already

said what light will say

Waitemata grey—

the ferry parts it gently,

gently it returns

snow on the loading

ramp, perfect, no boot has crossed—

morning is like that

I wore it eight years

before I left—the badge shows

nothing of the ward

bare oaks in the fog,

their branches the kind of grey

that is almost blue
