The rhythm mimics the rickshaw's bumpy progress through impossible conditions. ### [#64] "Monsoon sheet metal..." This haiku transforms the harsh reality of tin roofing into a percussion concert. The personification of darkness as a writer is Chen's masterstroke here. While humans lose the ability to write (no electric light), darkness gains agency and begins composing. There's something both ominous and beautiful about darkness as author, suggesting that absence can be as creative as presence. The midnight timing adds to the literary atmosphere—the traditional hour for both writers and supernatural activity. ### [#209] "Iowa snow fell..." The image of childhood being carried to the sea through storm drains is both melancholy and inevitable.
