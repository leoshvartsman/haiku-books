Between Pulse and Breath

Parker Lewis
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Between pulse and breath lies the space where we learn to tend—to others, to ourselves, to the places that shape us. This collection traces a journey from the sun-baked streets of Austin to the monsoon-washed wards of Kolkata, following the path of a caregiver who discovers that healing happens not just in hospitals, but in the quiet moments between heartbeats, in the texture of morning light on unfamiliar walls, in the taste of chai that becomes more familiar than childhood. These haiku map the geography of belonging—how we carry our origins in muscle memory while learning to call new places home. They explore the sacred ordinary of medical practice: the fluorescent-lit vigils, the small mercies of morphine and marigolds, the way a stethoscope becomes both tool and talisman. In the rhythm of seventeen syllables, we find the same rhythm that sustains life itself—the eternal conversation between what we leave behind and what we choose to carry forward.

Flour tortilla

wrapped around scrambled egg dreams—

dawn tastes like coming home

imperfections make it whole—

lesson number one

Ceiling fan blades slice

humid air into portions

I can finally breathe

Hospital corridor

endless under fluorescent suns

that never set

Marigold petals

drift past the ghats—I follow

their orange journey

New shoots emerge from

the banyan's ancient embrace—

youth wearing old skin
