Fleeting Landscapes

Skyler Johnson
160 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I look back on the places that have shaped me, I'm reminded of the fleeting nature of beauty itself. A moment's pause in a sun-drenched San Francisco morning, and the light is gone, leaving only the faintest whisper of its presence. It's this ephemeral quality that has drawn me to haiku – a form that captures the essence of experience before it slips away. These poems are my attempts to bottle the fragrance of foggy mornings on Haight Street, to distill the vibrant colors of Austin's street art, and to convey the mystical allure of Kathmandu's ancient temples. Each place has left an indelible mark on me, but the memories themselves remain fragile, like sandcastles at high tide. In these haiku, I've tried to recreate the sensory details that made each city feel like home – from the sound of mariachi music in Bogotá to the scent of incense in Kathmandu's alleys. As I revisit these landscapes in verse, I'm reminded that even as we leave one place behind, another beckons us forward, waiting to be explored and cherished.
