Papers in the Rain

Sky Ali
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A life requires so much paper. Birth certificates and school records, crossing documents and work permits, insurance forms and legal declarations, the slow accumulation of proof that you were here, that you were who you said you were, that you belonged somewhere on the earth. Sky Ali has spent seven decades gathering this paper, losing it, translating it, signing it in names that fit and names that didn't, and finally — in the last act of a long bureaucratic drama — choosing a name that does. Papers in the Rain is the record of that passage: three countries, two languages, one self, arriving late and intact. Haiku has always known how to hold what official documents cannot. Where a form demands a checkbox, a haiku offers a gecko pausing mid-step on white plaster. Where a policy schedule reduces a man to a bracket between two numbers, a haiku remembers that he sold fish each dawn. This collection trusts the form's ancient compression to carry what the paperwork leaves out — the blue exhaust of a father's motorbike, the sound of a ferry horn swallowed by grey harbour, the one inch a tissue box travels across a desk toward someone else's grief. These are not small things. They are the things that survive. The seven sections of this book move roughly in time, from a Saigon childhood luminous with mango light through decades of claiming and being claimed — by Sydney, by an industry of risk, by a new name, by San Francisco fog — to a late stillness that is not quite peace and not quite loss but something more honest than either. Readers who have carried their own papers through the world will recognise the particular exhaustion and dark comedy of proving you exist. Readers who have not may find, in these small careful poems, a door left open for them.
