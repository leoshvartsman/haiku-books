Soil Beneath Every Sky

Morgan Martinez
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Some books are made at a desk. This one was made in fields — in the cracked caliche of a Texas childhood, in the steam-laced streets of a city that dared you to become something, in the fog-softened furrows of a French farm where the work asked nothing of your ambitions and everything of your hands, in the rain-soaked basalt of a harbour city at the bottom of the world. Morgan Martinez has spent a life moving toward and away from ground, and these poems are the record of what the ground remembered even when the poet tried to forget. Haiku, with its insistence on the present tense, on the single image, on the breath between seeing and meaning, turned out to be the only form equal to that kind of attention. This collection does not argue for rootedness, nor does it mourn displacement. What it proposes, quietly and with dirt still under its fingernails, is something more difficult and more generous: that the sacred does not live in any particular soil but in the act of kneeling toward it, season after season, wherever you find yourself standing. Tending is the practice. Attention is the liturgy. The furrow is always, in some sense, the same furrow. Read these poems slowly, the way you would walk a row — not to reach the end, but to notice what is growing beside you. Each section marks a landscape and a season of a life, but the boundaries between them are porous, the way all borders between selves are porous. Dust from Texas turns up in a Paris zipper. A Hudson October echoes in an Auckland downpour. By the final poem, the map is not a map of places. It is a map of the hands that touched them.
