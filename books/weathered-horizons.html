Weathered Horizons

Sam Kim
168 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
I've always been drawn to the way moments can shift and change with the seasons. As a child in Auckland, I'd watch the sun rise over the harbor, painting the sky with hues of gold and pink. Later, in Manchester's misty greyness, I felt like an outsider, but found solace in the quiet rhythms of the city. These haikus are fragments of my own weathered horizons – snapshots of places that have shaped me, from the vibrant chaos of Beirut to the familiar comforts of Philadelphia. I've tried to capture the sensory details that linger: a scent of salt and seaweed, the taste of cardamom coffee, or the sound of traffic on a busy street. In this collection, I invite you to join me in exploring the quiet moments that connect us to our surroundings – moments that can be both fleeting and enduring.
