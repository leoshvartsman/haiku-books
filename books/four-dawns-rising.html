Four Dawns Rising

Quinn Smith
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Four Dawns Rising traces the geography of a life lived between languages, continents, and the shifting definitions of home. These haiku emerge from the spaces between—between childhood and adulthood, between leaving and arriving, between the names we inherit and the ones we choose. Each poem is a small meditation on displacement and belonging, capturing moments when the familiar becomes foreign and the foreign slowly transforms into something resembling home. Like the four dawns that mark the cardinal directions, this collection moves through the landscapes of memory: from the volcanic shores of New Zealand through the urban sprawl of Melbourne, into the flour-dusted rhythms of an Indian village, across the Mediterranean's ancient stones, and finally to the acceptance that home might not be a place but a practice—the daily ritual of making meaning from the fragments of all the places we've loved and left behind.

Crimson petals fall

into tide pools, becoming

boats for hermit crabs

City tram lurches—

my pen skips across the page,

making accidental art.

Kneading becomes prayer—

push fold turn push fold turn push

until muscles know

Monsoon's first downpour

drums on roof like eager hands

kneading sky to earth

Market stall awning

flaps in the morning sea wind—

Arabic numbers

Coffee grounds settle

as the first bus rumbles past

my unfinished verse
