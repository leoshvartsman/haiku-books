Every Departure Still Here

Robin Harris
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a particular kind of homesickness that has no fixed address — the kind that accumulates across years and latitudes until you carry not one lost place but several, layered and overlapping, each one pressing against the others like pages in a book left out in the rain. Robin Harris spent two decades moving between an outer-borough childhood, a rented room on the Pacific coast, the monsoon-soaked streets of Kolkata, and the vertical harbour of Hong Kong, writing software by day and, eventually, poems at night. Every Departure Still Here is what that accumulation looks like when you finally stop running long enough to write it down. These haiku do not proceed as a memoir would, with causality and clean chronology. They proceed the way memory actually does: associatively, stubbornly, with the body leading the mind. A particular quality of light calls back a fire escape. A monsoon's percussion becomes a debugging log. The smell of marigolds crushed into asphalt refuses to stay in Kolkata. Harris works in the haiku tradition's devotion to the sensory and the immediate, but the form here carries a double exposure — every present moment is also a palimpsest, the old landscapes visible beneath the new ones, none of them erased. The title is not a lament. It is a precise description of how place works on a person. Readers will find seven sections that move roughly outward and then inward again: from the borough rooftops of childhood through the disorientation of early displacement, the overwhelm and eventual belonging of immersion abroad, the dry interior weather of long hours at a screen, the suspended grace of transit, the alert stillness of a city learned late, and finally the strange, bittersweet peace of a self that has stopped trying to choose between its own geographies. You do not need to have lived in any of these cities to recognise what Harris is mapping. You need only to have ever left somewhere and felt it leave you back.
