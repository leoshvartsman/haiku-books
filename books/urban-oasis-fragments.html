Urban Oasis Fragments

Dakota Young
167 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
In the sweltering heat of a Hong Kong summer, I find myself drawn to fragments of nature amidst the concrete jungle. As an Angeleno transplanted in this vibrant city, I've grown accustomed to seeking solace in unexpected pockets of green – a rooftop garden, a bustling market's hidden alleys, or the tranquil banks of a forgotten harbor. It is here, in these fleeting glimpses of beauty, that I find my own sense of place. These haiku are snapshots of life lived between two worlds: the California girl in me yearns for sun-kissed hills and desert blooms, while the Hong Konger's edge whispers secrets of ancient wisdom and resilience. In them, you'll find echoes of memories made and moments lost – like a rain-soaked afternoon in Los Angeles, or the intoxicating aromas of a night market on Queen's Road Central. As I wander these city streets, camera-less and yet photographing every moment, I am reminded that poetry is not just a reflection of our experiences but also a way to weave them together into something new. These haiku fragments – some wistful, others defiantly vibrant – are my attempts to capture the ephemeral intersections between nature and urbanity, tradition and innovation.
