What Remains When Water Moves

Quinn Novak

Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of attention that forms under pressure — not the soft attention of contemplation, but the hard, narrow focus of a child learning to read a room for danger, a laborer reading soil for water, a displaced person reading a new language for the exits. Quinn Novak's second collection is made of that attention. These haiku do not seek transcendence. They stay low, close to the crack in the plaster, the blister opening on its own schedule, the fish sauce hitting the pan. They are interested in what actually happens, which is usually small and unremarkable and does not resolve. What Remains When Water Moves follows a life across geographies — a childhood room, North African fields, Casablanca irrigation ditches, a Baltimore kitchen in February, monsoon streets without names in any language the speaker fully owns — but it refuses the consolations of the journey narrative. Nothing is arrived at. Languages are learned and remain cages. Grief comes through smell rather than ceremony. The self that moves through these pages is not a self in the process of being understood; it is a self still in motion, still cataloguing, still counting the sounds the way monks count beads, which is to say: as a way of surviving the present moment rather than transcending it. Haiku, as a form, has always known something about impermanence — but it has sometimes been too comfortable with it, too ready to find the falling leaf beautiful. Novak is not interested in that comfort. These poems watch things dissolve and stay to describe the dissolving: the shape of it, the smell, the precise degree of turn before the blister opens. What remains when water moves is the channel. These poems are the channel.
