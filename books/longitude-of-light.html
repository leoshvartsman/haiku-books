jacaranda stains

the hot bitumen purple —

my bare feet learn this

before the school bell

a carpet of fallen bells

softens the asphalt

she sweeps the driveway

jacaranda falls again —

she sweeps, it falls, she

the uniform rule:

no shorts above the knee—

January disagrees

my bedroom window

frames a sky with no Orion

standing where he should

I look for the cross

through the louvers every night—

it moves without me

steam column rising

straight up in the still cold air —

I watch it until

geese prints in thin frost

lead to the edge of the path

and then just: water

the sewer grate steams

I stand over it in boots

I bought that morning

I find the function

in the call’s repeated phrase—

the engineer thinks

mint springs from a jar,

water clouding around the stems—

the vendor moves on

rooftop water tanks,

painted blue, catching nothing—

the sky does the work

Casablanca roof —

I knew the name of that dish

in three languages once

the cursor blinks on —

the function still expects me

to finish the thought

bougainvillea:

forty-seven open tabs,

one returns a thorn

the inner harbour

repeats the city in ice

less accurately

one candle per week—

I have not miscounted yet—

the dark is also math

Djurgården oak

still holding three brown leaves—

the elk ignore it
