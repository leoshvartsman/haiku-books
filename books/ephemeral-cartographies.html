Ephemeral Cartographies

Sam Patel
165 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit with these haiku, scattered across pages like fragments of a forgotten map, I'm reminded of the way memories cling to places. A smell can transport me back to my childhood in Montevideo's misty dawn, where the sea air would wrap itself around my skin. Or, it could be the taste of eucalyptus leaves that brings me to Sydney's summer haze, when the sun-kissed streets seemed to stretch on forever. In these haiku, I've tried to chart the contours of my global upbringing – not just the cities themselves, but the people and stories woven into their fabric. There's something ephemeral about the way memories settle over places, like a layer of dust that can be blown away with the slightest breeze. Yet, it's precisely this transience that gives them power. This collection is an attempt to pin down those elusive moments, to hold them up against the light and see what they reveal. It's not a straightforward mapping, but rather a cartography of the self – one that acknowledges the fluidity of identity, the way it shifts like sand between our fingers.
