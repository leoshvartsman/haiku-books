Liminal Landscapes

Morgan Kim
175 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I wander through cities, observing the intersections of culture and history, I find myself drawn to the liminal spaces where old meets new, where tradition blends with innovation. These landscapes, often overlooked in favor of grand monuments or scenic vistas, hold a depth and complexity that speaks directly to my own experiences as an urban dweller. In this collection, Liminal Landscapes, I've sought to capture the essence of these threshold areas – where streets become stages for human drama, and buildings stand as witnesses to generations past. Through haiku, a traditional form born of natural observation and distilled emotion, I aim to distill the intricate textures and rhythms of city life. As I weave together fragments of personal memory and urban narrative, I invite readers to join me in navigating these liminal landscapes – from wheat fields to rooftops, rainy nights to dusty storms. Here, where cultures converge and histories entwine, lies a world of beauty, tension, and transformation waiting to be explored.
