The Distance Between Names

Aarav Reddy
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A name is not a label. It is a sediment layer, a scar, a door left ajar in a house you no longer live in. This collection began as an attempt to map four cities — Delhi, New York, Accra, Copenhagen — and became, in the writing, an attempt to map the self that moved between them: what it accumulates, what it sheds, what it mistakes for permanence. The haiku form was not chosen for its brevity but for its honesty. It does not allow you to explain yourself. It asks you to place one thing beside another and trust the reader to feel the distance, or the closeness, or the strange way those two conditions can be identical.
