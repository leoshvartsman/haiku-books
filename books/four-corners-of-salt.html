Four Corners of Salt

Harper Brown
249 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Home is not a single place but a constellation of moments—the weight of red earth between your toes, the syncopated rhythm of city traffic, the precise silence of a laboratory at dawn. These haiku trace the geography of a life lived across continents, where memory becomes the thread connecting disparate landscapes into a single, breathing map of belonging. From the baobab shadows of West Africa to the crystalline winters of Scandinavia, from the salt-touched winds of North Africa's coast to the layered scents of family kitchens, 'Four Corners of Salt' follows the jazz rhythms of displacement and discovery. Each poem is a waypoint in the journey toward understanding that we carry all our homes within us—that the essence of place lives not in coordinates but in the sensory details that root themselves so deeply in our bodies they become indistinguishable from breath itself.

Sample Haiku

Baobab shadows

stretch across the red clay path—

grandmother's footsteps

Jazz club door swings wide—

bass notes tumble to sidewalk,

mixing with car horns

Stockholm's white nights burn

through blackout curtains while cells

divide in the dark

The hotel Bible

is written in Arabic—

I read it anyway

Orange trees in bloom

lean over garden walls where

fountains speak in drops

Storm cannot dim this

lighthouse beam that guides ships home—

steadfast as your kiss
