Borders of the Mouth

Sage García
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A border is never only a line on a map. It is also the pause before you answer the question of where you are from, the query mark penciled beside a word you chose with care, the gate that opens for a bird and closes for a person. Sage García has spent a lifetime crossing such gates — as a child in Tel Aviv, as a student and editor in America, as a witness in Delhi, as a late arrival in Manchester — and this collection is the record of what that crossing does to language, to loyalty, and to the body that carries both. 'Borders of the Mouth' is haiku as political autobiography: 250 poems arranged not as confession but as evidence, each one a small room with a large window. The haiku form is not incidental here. Its compression mirrors the compression of the checkpoint, the redacted line, the official form that offers no box for what you actually are. But García uses that compression the way a practiced editor uses silence — not to erase but to make the surrounding words ring louder. Across four languages and four landscapes, the collection asks what it means to belong to a place, a tongue, a tradition of witness, when all four of those things have different answers on different days. It does not resolve that question. It insists, instead, on the dignity of living inside it. Readers should know that this book moves — from the luminous bewilderment of childhood through intellectual hunger, immersive devotion, bureaucratic grief, and hard-won settlement, arriving finally at the unflinching clarity of a 69-year-old who has watched the same injustices cycle and has chosen, still, to name them. It is not a comfortable book. It is, in the way of the best haiku, an honest one: each poem a small gate, left open, a bird going through first.
