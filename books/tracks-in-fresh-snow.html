# Welcome to Sixty-Three Moments Welcome to this collection of haiku—sixty-three brief encounters with the natural world and the quiet moments that shape our days. Like dewdrops catching morning light or the last leaf clinging to an autumn branch, each poem here captures a single instant, inviting you to pause and see the world with fresh eyes. Haiku, the beloved Japanese poetic form, distills experience into its essence. Traditionally composed of seventeen syllables arranged in three lines (5-7-5), haiku seeks to present a moment of awareness—often featuring nature, the seasons, or sudden insights that arise from careful observation. The best haiku work like pebbles dropped into still water, creating ripples of meaning that extend far beyond their brief lines. This collection honors both the classical spirit of haiku and its evolution in contemporary poetry. You'll find poems that follow traditional patterns alongside others that embrace a more flexible approach to syllable count while maintaining haiku's essential quality: the ability to stop time and illuminate the extraordinary within the ordinary. The sixty-three haiku gathered here unfold through the seasons, beginning with spring's tentative awakening and cycling through summer's abundance, autumn's transformation, and winter's contemplative stillness. This seasonal organization reflects haiku's deep connection to the natural world and the Japanese concept of *mono no aware*—the bittersweet awareness of the impermanence of all things.
