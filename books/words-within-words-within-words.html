Words Within Words Within Words

Sunny Fields
375 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Every word contains a smaller word. Every silence contains the word that almost was. This collection began with a single observation — that language is not a flat surface but a set of nested chambers, each one opening into another, each one holding something that the outer layer cannot quite say. Sunny Fields has spent years listening for the moment before speech, the pause inside the pause, the meaning that lives in the white space around a syllable. These haiku are the record of that listening. The poems move in seven stages, from the raw hush of anticipation through the body's wordless knowing, through wonder and grief and the strangeness of translation, toward a final silence that is not empty but full — the silence of a bell that has already rung, still shaping the air around it. Together they ask a question that cannot be answered in a single language: what is a word, finally, but a shape the mouth makes around something it cannot hold? Read slowly. Read again. The collection is structured like the thing it describes — each section contains the next, and the last poem folds back quietly into the first. There is no definitive center. That is the point.
