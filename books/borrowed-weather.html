Borrowed Weather

Jun Seo
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of weather that does not belong to any forecast — the weather of other people's cities, other people's seasons, borrowed and worn like a coat that almost fits. Jun Seo has lived inside that weather for decades, moving through Busan, New York, Kyoto, and Mumbai, accumulating not a single home but a layered one, assembled from fish-market mornings and subway reflections and monsoon air that arrived before any word for it did. These haiku are the record of that accumulation — not a memoir exactly, and not a lament, but something closer to a field notebook kept by a self that refused to stop being curious about its own formation.

the cherry petals that reach

the water, and go

understanding all

and belonging to nothing —

the gate agent smiles
