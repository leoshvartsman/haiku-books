Before the City Wakes

Cameron Kim
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is an hour before the city wakes when the world belongs, briefly, to whoever is paying attention. Cameron Kim has spent a life paying attention in that hour — on a Tel Aviv shore where limestone dust settled into childhood feet, in the hushed reading rooms of Zurich where card catalogues held their silence like a held breath, on Tokyo streets where a single konbini awning burned yellow against the dark. These haiku are dispatches from that recurring hour, gathered across decades and continents into something that feels, finally, like a single sustained act of looking. The collection moves as attention moves — not in a straight line but in a series of arrivals. Each poem is the moment of touching down somewhere: in a memory, in a language that doesn't quite fit, in the specific gravity of an old book's back cover. Kim works in the classical haiku tradition's faith that the particular is the only path to the universal, that falafel grease on newsprint and ink the shade of over-steeped tea can carry more than abstraction ever could. The poems ask nothing of the reader except to stop, briefly, and notice that they too have been stopping and noticing all along. What emerges across these two hundred and fifty haiku is a quiet argument: that stillness is not the absence of motion but its most concentrated form. The speaker of these poems is always in transit — between languages, between cities, between the person who left and the person who arrived — and yet each poem is a moment of perfect rest inside that motion. To read this book is to practice what it teaches: the discipline of arriving, again and again, in the present tense.
