Between the Measures

Lou Bernard
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A self is not a fixed thing. It is a series of translations — from city to city, language to language, name to name — and something is always lost in the crossing, and something unexpected arrives in its place. Between the Measures is the record of one such life, assembled across four cities and four languages over the course of several decades, each haiku a small act of attention paid to the world as it appeared in a particular place, at a particular hour, before the next departure made it strange. The title borrows from music because music understood this first: that meaning lives not only in the notes but in the silences between them, in the breath before the phrase resolves, in the moment when the structure holds and the improvisation happens anyway. The haiku form asks for precision and accepts mystery. It does not explain. It places two things near each other and trusts the reader to feel the current that runs between them. This felt, from the beginning, like the right instrument for a life spent navigating the gap between what can be said in one language and what can only be gestured at in another. Each of these poems is a gesture. Each section is a city, or a state of being, or both — because cities and states of being are not so different when you have lived inside enough of them. Readers who have moved through the world carrying more than one version of themselves will find familiar ground here. So will those who have not — because the particular loneliness of a late desk lamp, a closed bakery, a train platform in the rain belongs to everyone eventually. These poems do not ask for sympathy. They ask for the same quality of attention they were written with: quiet, unhurried, willing to sit with what does not resolve.

the melody hesitates—

chooses the wrong door

for eleven hours only—

the Isar ran green,
