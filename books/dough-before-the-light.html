Dough Before the Light

Ananya Gupta
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a knowledge the hands hold that the mind cannot always name. It lives in the pressure of a thumb against dented steel, in the particular angle of a wrist over a bowl of warm water, in the way a body remembers — across decades and time zones and reinventions — exactly how much flour is enough. Dough Before the Light is a collection built from that knowledge: the body's quiet, stubborn archive of everywhere it has been and everyone it has been called. These haiku follow one woman across three cities and as many selves — from the dense, tender mornings of a Mumbai chawl, through the fog-lit solitude of a Brussels bakery, into the wide, jacaranda-scattered light of Nairobi. They move through the forms that asked for her name and the forms she made with her hands, through languages that fit and languages that didn't, through the long held breath of becoming and the first real exhale of being seen. They do not move in a straight line. Neither does a life. Ananya Gupta writes in the tradition of haiku as witness — not to nature alone, but to the nature of a woman moving through the world with her whole body paying attention. The moments here are small by design. A dented tiffin. A margin touched by another writer's grief. Cardamom splitting its skin with nobody watching. This is a collection that trusts the small thing to hold the large one, that believes a cupped palm is a complete philosophy, that knows the hour before the city wakes belongs, finally, to whoever was awake enough to claim it.

her thumb over the dent

the departure board

flips to Brussels — I watch it

flip to something else

yeast bloom, small and slow—

not miracle, just patience

given the right warmth

linden seeds in May—

I thought they were maple keys,

Mumbai had neither

red pen on my draft

a slash where I loved too much

the page breathes better

petals in my shoe

I walked through the tree without

meaning to receive it
