The Body Keeps the Latitude

Harper Martin
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A life can be measured in years, or it can be measured in coordinates. Casey Müller chooses coordinates. Across seven sections and more than two hundred haiku, The Body Keeps the Latitude traces a life lived between languages and latitudes — Stockholm's archipelago winters, Reykjavik's volcanic silences, London's tidal mud, Paris's rain-soaked gardens — each city pressing itself into the body like a thumb into wax, leaving an impression that neither time nor distance quite erases. This is a book about what sediments: the particular yellow of a Swedish streetlamp, the smell of the Thames still haunting a coat in March, the sound of Stockholm snow, which is different, the poet insists, from the sound of any other snow on earth.
