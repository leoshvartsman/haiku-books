Between Tides

Riley Wilson
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Movement shapes us in ways we rarely pause to notice. The rhythm of windshield wipers against Portuguese rain, the particular silence of snow falling on Montreal streets, the humid weight of Miami air pressing against car windows—each place we inhabit leaves its mark, not just in memory but in the cadence of our breath, the way we hold our shoulders, the languages that surface unbidden in moments of surprise or tenderness. These haiku trace the geography of a life lived between tides—the constant pull between staying and leaving, between the comfort of the familiar and the electric uncertainty of the new. From frost-edged mornings in New England to the sun-bleached boulevards of South Florida, from cobblestone climbs in Lisbon to the dashboard meditations of long drives, this collection maps the territory where belonging becomes not a destination but a practice. In the space between languages, between seasons, between the cities that shape us, we discover that home is not a place we find but a rhythm we learn to carry.

Sample Haiku

Morning frost collects

on maple leaves still clinging—

last night's argument

Library carrel holds

my shape after I've gone home—

ghost of late studying

Tram conductor waves

from his perch—we drivers have

our own sign language

Windshield wipers sweep

away the morning's small sins

with each gentle arc

Mispronounced "saudade"—

the feeling has no English

but my tongue keeps trying

Lightning illuminates

one second of clarity—

then darkness returns
