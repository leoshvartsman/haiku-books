Sandstorms In My Bones

Quinn Martin
159 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I look back on my life's journey, I see a trail of sandstorms etched into my bones – remnants of the places I've wandered to, and the people who've shared their stories with me along the way. From the arid expanses of North America to the snow-covered vastness of Europe, and from the scorching cities of South America to the ancient mystique of Africa, each step has left its mark on my soul. In this collection, I invite you to join me on that migratory path, to breathe in the desert hues of a Tucson sunrise, to feel the first snowflakes of a European winter, and to sway with the rhythm of tango nights in Buenos Aires. We'll wander through the gardens of Alhambra, where Moorish whispers still linger in the air, and pause by the Nile's banks, where pharaohs once reigned. These haiku are not mere snapshots; they're fragments of my own story, woven with the tales of those I've met and loved along the way. They capture moments of wonder, loss, and belonging – fleeting glimpses into a life lived in transit, yet somehow anchored by the people, landscapes, and experiences that have become an integral part of me.
