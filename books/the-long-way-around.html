The Long Way Around

Quinn Wilson
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of travel that has nothing to do with distance. It happens in the body first — in the soles of the feet on unfamiliar pavement, in the nose catching something that shouldn't be familiar but is, in the hands reaching for a door handle in the dark and finding it exactly where it was supposed to be. The Long Way Around is a record of that travel: not the grand arc of immigration or reinvention, but the granular, sensory, often comic negotiation of becoming someone in a series of places that didn't ask you to arrive and didn't ask you to stay. These haiku were written across four decades and four cities — Rome, San Francisco, Portland, Philadelphia — though they resist the logic of the itinerary. They are organized not as a travelogue but as a kind of stratigraphic map, each section a layer of sediment laid down by a particular season of life. The smell of basalt at dawn and the smell of ginkgo fruit on a November sidewalk are not opposites; they are the same nose, learning. Identity, this collection proposes, is not a destination you arrive at but a residue that accumulates — on the coat, in the notebook, under the fingernail, at the back of the throat. Haiku, with its insistence on the present moment and its suspicion of explanation, turned out to be the right form for this kind of accounting. A haiku cannot argue. It can only show you the espresso growing cold on the wrong side of the table and trust you to feel the time zone. That trust — between the image and the reader, between the self who left and the self who stayed — is what this book is finally about.
