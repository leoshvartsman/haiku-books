Pulse at the Wrist of the World

Alex Pérez
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A paramedic learns quickly that time is not a river moving in one direction but a pulse — rhythmic, interruptible, and always asking to be read. Alex Pérez has spent a life reading it: in the blue compression of a resuscitation, in the slow purple fall of a jacaranda, in the long suspension of a transatlantic cabin going dark. 'Pulse at the Wrist of the World' is the record of that reading — 250 haiku assembled across four cities, three languages, and the countless unnamed places in between, where a siren has just stopped and the real work is beginning. The haiku form, with its ancient insistence on the present moment and its seasonal markers called kigo, turns out to be the ideal instrument for a life built from arrivals and departures. Pérez does not use the seasons as nostalgia. He uses them as a portable home — a way of staying oriented when every other coordinate has shifted. A jacaranda in Buenos Aires and a jacaranda in Mexico City are not the same tree, but they ask the same question of the body standing beneath them. These poems are the body's answers, given quickly, the way you have to answer in an emergency: with whatever is in your hands. The collection moves through seven sections that follow a rough chronology without pretending that a life is ever truly linear. Childhood, crisis, wonder, displacement, grief, and something that might finally be called belonging — each arrives in its own weather. Readers who have ever felt the particular vertigo of loving a place they have left, or the strange calm of being needed somewhere unfamiliar, will find their own pulse taken here, gently, in the dark.
