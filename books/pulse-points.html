Pulse Points

Parker Lewis
250 haiku
The stethoscope becomes a compass in these pages, guiding us through the intimate cartography of a traveling nurse whose decade-long journey spans four countries and countless moments of human connection. Parker Lewis maps the pulse points not just of patients, but of places—the swimming holes of Texas childhood, the temple bells of awakening consciousness, the monsoon-soaked clinics where healing happens in languages the heart understands before the mind catches up. These haiku capture the precise instant where clinical observation transforms into tenderness, where the practiced eye of a healthcare worker meets the wondering gaze of a perpetual traveler. From the taco truck shade of memory to the currency exchanges of restless transition, each poem locates that exact pressure point where personal and professional, foreign and familiar, converge in seventeen syllables that pulse with their own vital rhythm.
