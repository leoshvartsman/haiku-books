Luminous Migrations

Sol Gómez
138 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I reflect on the threads that weave together this collection of haikus, I'm reminded of the countless moments when the boundaries between self and surroundings blurred. A childhood spent chasing sunbeams in Madrid's streets, a young adulthood lost in the vast expanse of California skies, a wanderlust that carried me to distant shores – each place a chapter in my own story of migration. These poems are fragments of a life, gathered from moments when I paused to breathe in the beauty and complexity of the world around me. The rhythms of nature – wind, water, earth – became an underlying cadence, underscoring the ebb and flow of human experience. In this collection, I've tried to capture the luminous edges where memory meets landscape, where people meet place. In the following pages, you'll find snapshots of various journeys: a quiet morning in Lisbon's gardens, the vibrant nights of Austin, the unmarked borders that separate us from ourselves and one another. These haikus are not merely observations; they're fragments of my own migration, a search for meaning in the ever-changing tapestry of life.
