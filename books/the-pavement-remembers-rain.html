The Pavement Remembers Rain

Aditya Reddy
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Aditya Reddy has lived in cities the way water lives in stone — not on the surface, but inside. Over nine decades of walking, selling, watching, and returning, he has moved through the streets of Delhi, Cairo, and a dozen cities between them, and somewhere along the way he stopped distinguishing between the city outside and the city he carries. This collection is the record of that confusion, and its quiet joy. The pavement, he has found, does not forget. Neither does the body. Neither does the man who has learned to trust them both. These haiku were written in the poet's ninety-fourth year, and they carry that fact without announcing it — in the patience of their attention, in the way they linger where a younger writer might move on, in the tenderness they extend to weight, to wear, to the particular dignity of things that have lasted. To read them in order is to walk a single long street: beginning in the physical world of concrete and root, moving through memory and labor and heat and language, and arriving, finally, at a desk where a hand is still moving across a page, unhurried, casting its shadow, unafraid. Reddy does not write about the past. He writes from inside an accumulated present, where every street contains every street he has ever walked, and the body itself has become the archive. What he offers the reader is not nostalgia but something rarer: the sensation of time as texture, as sediment, as the warm tarmac beneath a sleeping dog that holds, with perfect fidelity, this exact weight.

warm tarmac under

a sleeping dog — what concrete

holds: this exact weight

product photograph

glossy in the afternoon —

the café flies land

the collar knows sweat

before the wearer does — cloth

is its own witness

between sleep and Hindi

a door of Arabic light

I pass through both ways

the young man's briefcase

catches the afternoon light—

he does not notice

between two seasons

on a street I have walked forty

years: a new cold thing
