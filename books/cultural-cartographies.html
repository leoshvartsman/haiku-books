Cultural Cartographies

Casey Kim
187 haiku

As I reflect on the path that brought me to this collection, I'm reminded of the countless afternoons spent wandering Accra's markets, the hushed tones of Oxford's college chapels at twilight, and the snug warmth of Copenhagen's hygge-drenched cafes. These moments – fleeting, yet forever etched in memory – ignited a fascination with the intersections of culture, identity, and place. In "Cultural Cartographies," I attempt to capture the essence of these intersections through haiku, a form that has become both a challenge and a solace for me. By distilling experience into concise, three-line verses, I hope to convey the depths of emotion and connection that arise from our encounters with the world around us. This collection is not so much about mapping territories as it is about charting the contours of the human heart. As you turn these pages, I invite you to join me on a journey through the in-between spaces – where cultures blend, identities shift, and the self is redefined by every new encounter.
