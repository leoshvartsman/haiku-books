Between Every Signal

Alex Hoffmann
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of attention that only displacement teaches. When you have lived between languages, between riverbanks, between the city you were given and the cities you chose, you learn to notice what most people walk past: the particular green of glacial water, the way a room shifts when a bassist moves his hand up the neck, the exact moment fog releases a hillside back to itself. Alex Hoffmann has lived in that noticing. Born in Munich, schooled in Baltimore, briefly rooted in Dublin, and now suspended in the amber light of Los Angeles, he has spent years collecting the small evidence that a life is actually happening — that the body remembers what the mind is still trying to name. Between Every Signal is arranged the way a journey actually feels: not as a clean arc from there to here, but as a series of arrivals and departures that keep revising each other. The haiku form suits this perfectly. Each poem is a signal sent across a gap — between breath and speech, between the note and the silence that gives it shape, between München surfacing mid-sentence in a Los Angeles grocery store and the cashier's patient smile. Hoffmann learned from jazz that the space between notes is not empty. It is where meaning gathers before it becomes sound. These poems live in that space. Read slowly. Let the white space on the page do its work. These are not poems that explain displacement — they enact it, one precise image at a time, until you find yourself leaning, like everyone in the room when the bassist shifts his hand, one inch toward something you cannot quite name but suddenly, unmistakably, feel.
