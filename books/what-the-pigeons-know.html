What the Pigeons Know

Sol Vázquez
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Sol Vázquez has lived in four cities across nine decades, and this book is not about any of them. It is about the weed that splits the cobblestone, the bird that crosses six lanes of traffic without adjusting its wings, the frost that arrives on the river whether or not anyone has a word for it. What the Pigeons Know gathers haiku written across a lifetime of crossings — of borders, of languages, of seasons that arrived without introduction — and finds in each of them not a lesson but a fact: that the non-human world persists with a patience that puts our categories gently to shame. These poems move through childhood on a wide South American river, through the cold displacement of learning a new hemisphere's seasons, through the sun-bleached indifference of a freeway city, through the quiet disorientation of language losing its grip, and finally into the long, clear light of a life that has outlasted several of its own assumptions. Vázquez does not organize this journey as nostalgia. The tense throughout is present. The attention is steady and a little ruthless, the way attention must be when you have learned that nothing — not a city, not a language, not a body — stays the same container twice. Haiku has always been a form that trusts the particular over the general, the moment over the meaning. For a writer who has spent a lifetime translating between worlds, it is the natural form: small enough to hold one true thing, spacious enough to let that thing breathe. Read these poems slowly, or read them the way you watch a bird cross a sky — without trying to name it before it is gone.
