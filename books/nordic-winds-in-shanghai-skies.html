Nordic Winds In Shanghai Skies

River Kim
183 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I stand on the Bund, watching the Yangtze River flow beneath Shanghai's towering skyscrapers, I feel the winds of my past whispering through the city's steel and stone. Memories of childhood summers in Oslo, spent chasing fireflies in the midnight sun, blend with the vibrant streets of Amsterdam, where canals sang lullabies to the city's secrets. And yet, here I am, an immigrant's heart beating in this vast, cosmopolitan metropolis. This collection of haiku is a map of my fragmented landscapes – the places that have shaped me, and the selves I've discovered along the way. With each step into this volume, I invite you to join me on a journey through the intersections of culture, language, and identity. You'll find echoes of nostalgia in Oslo's quiet streets, the rhythms of Amsterdam's waterways, and the frenetic energy of New York City's skyscrapers. But amidst these familiar landscapes, I've woven threads from the more elusive – the memories of migration, the contours of language, and the liminal spaces where cultures intersect. In these haiku, I've sought to capture the ephemeral beauty of life in translation – the moments when words fail us, but the world still speaks its own quiet truth. This is a collection about finding one's way between worlds, and being found by the winds that carry us home.
