What the Dawn Remembers

Riley Kowalski
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Riley Kowalski wrote every poem in this collection in the hour before dawn — that particular darkness which is not quite night, when the mind loosens its grip on the present and the past moves freely through it. At ninety-three, Kowalski has lived inside four cities that could not be more unlike one another: Hanoi's verdant, steaming corridors; the wide, cold parishes of rural Iowa; Dhaka's teeming, rain-pressed streets; and Athens, where the light falls on stone that has already outlasted everything. These are not cities one accumulates. They accumulate you. They leave their weather inside your body, their languages tangled in your syntax, their particular quality of morning light permanently altering what you see when you close your eyes. This book is the record of that accumulation — not a memoir, not a travelogue, but something closer to what memory actually feels like: fragmentary, sensory, arriving without warning, and more honest than any sustained narrative could be.
