What the Body Remembers

Sky Smith
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of knowledge the body holds that language has not yet reached. It lives in the way a particular steam — milky, fishy, sweet — can collapse twenty years in an instant; in the way cold air entering a specific angle of window can return you to a city you swore you had left. What the Body Remembers is a collection built from that knowledge: haiku gathered across four countries, four languages, and the long, slow work of understanding that a self is not a single thing but an accumulation — of kitchens, of crossings, of names worn like different coats against different weathers. These poems move through Manila's tidal humidity, the disorienting brightness of coastal California, the austere white discipline of Montreal winters, and the salt-soaked, bougainvillea-stained light of Rio de Janeiro. They move, too, through the interior geographies that have no country: the ache of early formation, the quiet fracture of inherited names, the vertigo of becoming someone new in a language that does not yet have a word for what you are losing. The haiku form — spare, attentive, grounded in the sensory instant — felt like the only honest container for this kind of reckoning. A life lived across borders accumulates too much for narrative to hold cleanly. But a single heron standing still in the silt. A burn scar catching January light. These the form can carry. What you will find here is not a story of loss, though loss is present. It is not a story of arrival, though arrival comes. It is, more precisely, a cartography drawn by the body itself — the archive that travels with us when everything else is left behind — asking what we carry, what we shed, and what we become when we are finally alone with a knife, a flame, and the quiet authority of our own name.

outside, a dry wind

ice floes on the river—

each one alone, each one

going the same way

after the last guest,

the sommelier uncorks one

for the ones who cooked

purple, orange, red—

the bougainvillea does not

choose. It bleeds all three.
