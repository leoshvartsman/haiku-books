What the Body Remembers

Riley Müller
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of knowledge that never reaches the mind. It lives instead in the hands — in the particular angle of a wrist over a hot pan, in the way fingers learn to read a blade before the eyes do, in the muscle-memory of a childhood harbour that reasserts itself decades later in the smell of brine on newsprint. What the Body Remembers is a collection built from that knowledge: small, precise moments gathered across a life lived in kitchens and harbours, in cities that hum in languages the body must learn before the tongue catches up. Riley Müller has arranged these haiku not as autobiography but as archaeology — each section a different stratum, a different layer of skin shed and kept. Copenhagen to Shanghai to New Orleans, apprentice to craftsperson to someone learning, at twenty-six, to fluently think about endings. The haiku form suits this project with uncomfortable precision: seventeen syllables is roughly the amount of space grief or wonder or the smell of a braising pot will occupy before it becomes something else. These poems do not try to hold more than that. They hold exactly that. To read this collection is to be slowly, tenderly unmade alongside it. The education on offer here is not the kind that arrives as revelation — it arrives as repetition, as accumulation, as the slow copper-darkening of a braise left to work. You will not be the same reader at the end as you were at the beginning. The body, as Müller reminds us, keeps its own account.
