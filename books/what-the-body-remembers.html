the sun arrives flat

at every hour — no slant

no reason to doubt

volcano swallowed

by its own grey throat — still there

my mother says, still

hibiscus bundles

tied with plastic twine — red mouths

open toward nothing

Sato: four letters

I sounded out in a book

before I claimed them

waiting room plastic,

the chair’s seam against my thigh—

someone calls a name

kanji for “village”—

里—I traced it on glass

with my left finger

2 a.m., I type

the same first line seven times—

delete, delete, delete

eucalyptus oil

on my hands from touching bark—

I smell like elsewhere

orange tree in bloom

behind a chain-link section—

I press my face close

brand deck, slide eleven:

authenticity matters—

the purple falls still

tapatío heat

enters the taxi slowly,

settles in the back

the hour between things—

I stand near the flower cart

without buying one

the stranger’s gesture:

she touched the doorframe leaving —

habit or goodbye

a notebook page bent

at the corner means I stopped

mid-sentence for life

transit card, depleted:

I stand at the turnstile like

a man with a thought

the geese have opinions

about the waterfront path

I walk around them

fog so complete that

the tower could be anywhere—

it is probably here

snow that rhymes with snow

I knew but does not repeat it—

a near translation
