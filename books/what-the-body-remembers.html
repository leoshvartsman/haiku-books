What the Body Remembers

Parker Sato
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of knowledge the mind refuses to hold but the body keeps faithfully, the way a scar keeps the shape of what made it, the way a hand lifted toward warmth remembers every fire it has ever known. What the Body Remembers is a life written in latitudes — Bangkok and Toronto, the Swiss Alps and Osaka's rain-slicked streets — not as a travelogue but as an interior map, tracing the routes by which a person is made, unmade, and slowly, imperfectly, reconstituted. Sky García's haiku do not explain this life. They inhabit it, moment by moment, the way weather inhabits a country: arriving without announcement, leaving something altered behind.

the children's voices behind me —
