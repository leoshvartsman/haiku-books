Fractured Seasons

Taylor Hall
180 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I look back on the years spent wandering through seasons and places, I begin to see the threads of connection that weave them together – the mist-shrouded coasts of Maine, where sea spray kissed my skin; the vibrant streets of Baltimore, where rhythms pulsed like a living thing; the Kathmandu hills, where sun-kissed stone temples whispered ancient tales; and Beirut's bridges, straddling centuries. In these landscapes, I found fragments of myself – shards of identity forged in the crucible of place. This collection, Fractured Seasons, is an attempt to distill those moments into their essence. Within its pages, you'll find haikus that capture not just the beauty of these places, but also the dissonance and disorientation that can come with encountering the unfamiliar. It's a journey through seasons as much as it is through geography – from summer's languid heat to winter's snowy stillness. I've organized this collection into seven sections: five dedicated to specific landscapes, two to themes of transition and connection. In between, I've woven in smaller pieces that explore the liminal spaces where one place bleeds into another – kigo landscapes, if you will. These fragments are what I hope readers will find most resonant, for it's in these moments of dislocation and discovery that we often stumble upon something new about ourselves.
