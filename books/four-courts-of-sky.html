Four Courts of Sky

Cruz Flores
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
This collection traces a journey through four landscapes that shaped my understanding of justice—not as an abstract concept carved in courthouse stone, but as something alive, breathing through volcanic dust and mountain air, rooted in red clay and river bends. From Mexico City's jacaranda-lined streets where I first learned to read, through Virginia's piedmont awakening, to Brisbane's subtropical chambers where I wielded the gavel, and finally to Denver's thin air where verdicts echo differently—each place became a courtroom where memory, nature, and wisdom converged. These haiku emerged from daily walks, quiet moments between cases, and the recognition that justice, like seasons, follows patterns larger than our individual understanding. They are meditations on belonging—to place, to purpose, to the long tradition of seeking balance in an unbalanced world. In each court of sky, I found the same truth: that judgment begins with attention, and attention begins with standing still long enough to let the world speak its testimony.

Sample Haiku

Gray dust settles on

jacaranda petals—spring

in the valley's grip

Red clay stains my palm

like ochre paint—I want to

mark everything here

Gavel strikes once, twice—

the sound echoes in high halls

where justice sits still

Witness speaks slowly—

each word carries the weight of

storm clouds gathering

Thunder rolls distant

over suburban courtrooms

storm still miles away

Golden light catches

spider silk between two pines

afternoon's fine thread
