Rainy Afternoon Shadows

Cameron Brown

As I sit with pen in hand, I'm transported back to afternoons spent chasing raindrops on Jakarta's sidewalks. Memories of my childhood linger, a bittersweet mix of joy and longing. In this collection, Rainy Afternoon Shadows, I've sought to capture the essence of those formative years, when the rhythms of nature and city were forever intertwined. As life took me to Rome, and later Guadalajara, new landscapes unfolded before me. The rough-hewn beauty of carpentry, the heat-baked streets of a Mexican market – each place etched its own distinct character onto my soul. In this collection, I've woven together threads from these disparate experiences, allowing them to inform one another like the interconnected shadows on a rainy day. These haiku are more than mere snapshots; they're fragments of self, reflections of the ways in which place and memory shape us. With each line, I hope to evoke not only the sights and sounds of far-off cities but also the quiet moments when our past and present converge – a fleeting glimpse into the ever-changing tapestry of experience that is our life.
