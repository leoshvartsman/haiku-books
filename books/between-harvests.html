Between Harvests

Diya Shah
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Diya Shah's Between Harvests unfolds in the hours before dawn on a Tuscan hillside, where pre-dawn silence and agricultural time become the ground for a reckoning with diaspora, belonging, and the body's accumulated memories. The collection moves between the Arno valley and Kolkata, between fennel and cardamom, between the Italian word for farmer and the Hindi one—inhabiting the gap where no single language is quite adequate. These are poems of threshold: between sleep and waking, between seasons, between the self that left and the one that stayed.

from her, not toward — I’ve tried

as if approving

prego, prego —

in Hindi we say aao, aao —

the door opens twice

the aquifer holds

last century’s rain — I drink it
