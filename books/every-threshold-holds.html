Every Threshold Holds

Casey Smith
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Every threshold holds something — the cold of a door handle, the smell of a city not yet known, the particular silence before a new language begins in your mouth. This collection moves through four cities and four languages not as a travelogue but as a reckoning: with what displacement costs, what it quietly gives, and what the body insists on keeping long after the mind has declared itself settled. Casey Smith's haiku do not document places so much as they document the self in the act of crossing into them — the friction, the vertigo, the slow and sometimes startling moments of arrival. The book traces a life lived in accumulation: Zurich's grey precision, Manhattan's electric appetite, the ancient limestone light of a city in the Middle East, and finally Bogotá's altitude-drunk dailiness. Each city demanded a different kind of attention, a different posture of the senses, and the haiku collected here are the residue of that attention — small, exact, honest about what was lost in translation and what, surprisingly, was not. Language itself becomes a recurring threshold, a place where meaning hovers between one word and another, where the tongue holds two homes at once. Haiku, with its insistence on the present moment and the physical world, turns out to be the ideal form for a life lived in transit. There is no room in seventeen syllables for nostalgia to become indulgent, no space for displacement to become performance. What remains is only what the body actually registered: a tram bell, a crosswalk button already hot, the smell of cold mineral water released by a shower in a country far from the lake that made it. These poems ask what it means to belong to multiple places simultaneously — and suggest, gently, that the answer lives not in the mind's declarations but in the body's quiet, persistent memory.
