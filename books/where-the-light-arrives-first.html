salt on the back teeth —

the strait opens its grey mouth

before I can speak

my mother’s clothesline

snaps in the southerly —

shirts fly toward Petone

tūī song at six —

not music, something older,

throat-deep and private

Tuscany notary,

his stamp, my new syllables—

the ink dried so fast

between two surnames

a white space in the ledger

where I used to live

the hyphen I chose

became a scar I chose—

both are mine to keep

nets on the grove floor

catching moonlight now, the yield

already recorded

smoothed coin on the sill—

I use it as a paperweight

for the final draft

wine-dark ring on oak—

the table holds its own books,

stains I cannot close

what the split fig shows:

a chamber I never found

in any spreadsheet

zero is the bell

before the striking, the fig

before the splitting—

what I carried in:

what I carried out—the bell

counts neither correctly

saffron hem wet

from the crossing—monk walks on

as if cloth were air

tin roof, rain, 5am:

I was a numbers man—

now I count drops

spirit house jasmine

wound between the marigolds—

the smell arrives first

I set the glasses

on the mat because the floor

is where I still sit

reading glasses lost

then found under my left knee—

I was sitting still

I peel the mango

in one long unbroken strip—

some days I manage
