Salt on Three Shores

Young Lee
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
These poems were written across three coastlines and two decades, though the salt in the title belongs to no single shore. It belongs, instead, to a way of paying attention — to the moment before language arrives, when the body already knows something the mind is still reaching for. The haiku gathered here move between a Korean American childhood on the Pacific, years spent in the Mediterranean...

Sample Haiku

first light on the wharf—

the auctioneer’s hand cutting

through mackerel smoke

grandmother bends low

tapping the lid of each pot—

hollow, hollow, full

yellow dust on glass—

I pressed my thumb and watched it

bloom into a print

Hebrew in my throat,

Spanish behind my teeth—

neither one comes out

dawn window fogged—

the ocean erases itself

one wave at a time

new consonants sharp

against the roof of my mouth—

I swallow the word

the sea smell arrives

before the sea is visible—

salt teaches this way

pomegranate held

up to the afternoon light—

the seeds count themselves

Arabic spoken

over a fence to Hebrew—

the jasmine between

stars over the wadi

I came here with questions once—

now I come with less

the Negev at dusk

turns colors I have no name for

in any language

small lizard on stone

warming itself beside me—

we share this service

she rang from Busan

at midnight Santiago—

I heard her breakfast

a Korean word

surfaces in Hebrew prayer—

I swallow it whole

the photograph shows

a man I recognize

by his standing still

I am sixty-two

and still the mountains teach me

to stand and say nothing

jacaranda roots

split the pavement—beauty here

does not apologize

the condor adjusts

altitude without effort—

I watch and take notes
