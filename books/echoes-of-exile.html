Echoes Of Exile

Xia Zhao
187 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
In the silences between places, I've found a language that speaks to me. Haiku, with its syllabic cadence and quiet intimacy, has been my companion on journeys through cities, seasons, and cultures. It's a form that acknowledges the impermanence of things, even as it seeks to hold onto fragments of memory. As I wander through the echoes of exile – a term I use loosely, for home is not always a fixed point, but rather a constellation of moments, people, and places – I find myself drawn to the ephemeral beauty of haiku. In its simplicity lies a depth that speaks to the human experience: longing, dislocation, and the quest for belonging. In this collection, I've tried to distill the essence of those fragmented memories into haiku that capture the contours of my journey. Shanghai's narrow alleys give way to New York's midnight skies, Norway's wild coastlines, Hiroshima's bittersweet blooms, and the crossroads where memories intersect with time itself. In these poems, I invite you to join me on a path of rediscovery, to find solace in the impermanence that binds us all.
