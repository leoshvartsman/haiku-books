Sydney Sunsets To Tokyo Drift

Sam Brown
149 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit on the edge of a Tokyo rooftop, watching the city surrender to night's dark veil, I'm reminded of the countless sunsets I've witnessed elsewhere – in Sydney's harborside suburbs, on Miami's Art Deco beaches, and in hidden corners of cities yet to be discovered. Each fleeting moment has left an indelible mark on my soul. These haikus are a journey through the intersections of culture, identity, and place. They're born from the whispers of coastal towns, the neon-lit dreams of metropolises, and the quiet laments of those who've wandered between worlds. I've sought to capture the beauty in these moments – the way light dances across water's surface, or the whispered secrets shared over steaming bowls of ramen. In this collection, you'll find fragments of my own story woven alongside those of strangers encountered on distant shores. I invite you to join me as we navigate Liminal Spaces, where borders blur and our understanding of self is tested. In these haikus, I've aimed to distill the essence of a particular moment – one that lingers long after the sun dips below the horizon, leaving behind only echoes of what's been seen.
