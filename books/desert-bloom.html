Desert Bloom

Robin Kowalski
169 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
In the arid spaces of my mind, a garden has grown - a collection of haiku that flower from city streets to hidden memories. Desert Bloom is a reflection of my wanderings, where concrete and stone give way to whispers of the past. These poems are born from strolls through Cairo's bustling souks, where aromas of cardamom and ginger transport me back to childhood summers spent in my grandmother's kitchen. They bloom from the frost-kissed mornings of Vermont's winters, when the earthy scent of snowmelt awakens a sense of renewal. As I traveled from Auckland to Miami, I found fragments of myself embedded in each city's rhythm: the urban pulse of Auckland, where rain-soaked streets sing with secret lives; the vibrant modernity of Miami, where Art Deco façades shimmer like shards of sunlight. These haiku capture moments when culture and memory converge, and the desert within me begins to bloom.
