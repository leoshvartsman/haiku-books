Four Seasons, Four Tongues

Kim Schmidt
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Language is a geography of the heart. In these haiku, written across decades of movement between Berlin, Kyoto, Guadalajara, and Austin, I have tried to map the seasons that connect us all—the universal kigo that needs no translation. As a translator, I have spent my life building bridges between tongues, but it is in the spare architecture of seventeen syllables that I have found the truest crossings. These poems follow the ancient rhythm of seasonal observation while tracing a modern migration: departure gates and temple bells, monsoon rains and Texas heat, the weight of words that shift meaning as they cross borders. Each season returns, but never quite the same—like memory, like belonging, like the self that learns to see with new eyes in each landscape. Here are four seasons lived in four tongues, finding home in the spaces between languages.
