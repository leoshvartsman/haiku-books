Northern Light, Southern Winds

Alex Chen
121 haiku

I've spent most of my life torn between two hemispheres: where morning sun rises over Vermont's snow-kissed fields, and where it sets over Uruguay's rolling hills. My haiku collection, Northern Light, Southern Winds, is a map of these in-between places – the quiet moments when cultures blend, and landscapes merge. These poems were born from countless afternoons spent hiking through New England forests, listening to sea gulls cry overhead; from late nights huddled with friends in Seattle's dimly lit cafes, sipping coffee and swapping stories; from strolls along Montevideo's rambling coastline, where cacti meet the ocean. Each haiku is a fragment of memory, a nod to the land that shaped me. This collection is more than a travelogue – it's an exploration of the self in flux. How do we hold multiple identities? What happens when cultures intersect, and languages blend? In these haiku, I've tried to capture the moments of connection that form us, the spaces where belonging becomes possible.
