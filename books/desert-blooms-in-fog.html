Desert Blooms In Fog

Robin Lee
206 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit in stillness, surrounded by the whispers of my poems, I am reminded of the desert blooms that have guided me on this journey. In the fog-shrouded landscapes of my childhood, I first discovered the beauty of impermanence – a delicate flower unfurling its petals only to wither away just as suddenly. This paradox has been a constant companion, echoing through the years and continents I've traversed. From Cairo's golden light to Kyoto's subtle seasons, each place has left an indelible mark on my soul. The cities' rhythms – their noise, their beauty, their contradictions – have become a symphony that informs my words. Dubai's mirage-like oases and San Francisco's misty mornings have all contributed to this poetry of wonder. As I reflect on the threads that weave these poems together, I am struck by the power of nature to transcend borders, cultures, and time. In these haikus, I invite you to join me in embracing the fleeting moments of beauty that dot our lives. May they transport you to the crossroads where past and present meet, where memory and experience converge. Let us walk together through the seasons, attuned to the whispers of the natural world – and find solace in its wisdom.
