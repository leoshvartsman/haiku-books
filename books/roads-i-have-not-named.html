Roads I Have Not Named

Nikhil Khan
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Nikhil Khan has spent years behind the wheel — first on the potholed arteries of Delhi, then on the fog-narrowed boreens of Connacht, then on the rain-slicked overpasses of Portland, Oregon. This collection grew out of those long silences: the particular quality of attention a driver develops when the road asks nothing of you except to keep going. Haiku, it turns out, is the form that fits inside that silence. Brief, patient, awake to the physical world, it asks the same thing the road asks — presence, without demand for resolution.

dust on mango leaves—

my mother's hand wiping them

one by one, slow

terminal coffee

burns the same in every mouth—

I drink it standing

cracked dashboard saint—

between the cracks, older paint

a different colour

headlights on the ditch:

a fox considers the light,

then does not exist

static on the line

then her voice — then more static —

I keep saying yes

food cart naan smell lifts

something in my chest opens

then the wind changes
