Sample Haiku

wet wool on iron—

the radiator clicks once

and Oslo breathes out

tram bell in the dark,

sparks blue above the wire—

I count the stops home

birch bark peels back white

against the grey February—

one lamp in the school

rain on the canal:

each drop opens and closes

a mouth saying nothing

grey canal water—

the barge rope tightens, loosens,

tightens against stone

the radiator

knocks at three in the morning—

I do not wake her

the wallah pours chai

from height, a thin brown ribbon—

I watch it, not him

policy number

stamped in purple on pink sheet—

marigold outside

fan blade catches light

once per revolution—

I count without thought

claim form number six:

I stamp it blue and slide it

across his thin hands

December fog lifts

just enough to show the street

then swallows it whole

phone rings, no one there —

the office holds the echo

longer than I do

contrail over water,

already fraying at the edge

before I see it

the gate number changed—

I carry my single bag

to the new silence

Oslo, Manchester,

Delhi, Austin—the boarding pass

knows only one name

one grackle drops down,

finds nothing on the asphalt,

lifts without hurry

heat at ten o’clock—

the limestone wall already

holding yesterday

I drove the highway

slow enough to see them—blue

along the white edge
