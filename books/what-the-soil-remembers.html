cobblestone still warm

long after the sun has gone—

my palm reads the street

cobblestones after rain:

each joint dark as a vein,

the city bleeding calm

terracotta holds

the orange of its making

long past the sunset

Tiber evening,

I throw in nothing, take back

only the watching

pine shadow stretches

across stones no one has named—

the road walks itself

the blank field waits—

I press my pen to the page

and feel my pulse lift

library lamp

throws my hand into shadow—

I write in the dark half

damp coat on the hook

still holding the shape of rain

from the walk over

the elm holds nothing

its bare arms full of winter

and one distant rook

gecko flicks its tongue —

the monsoon shifts registers,

now a lower note

wet season morning:

the lab coat hangs like a flag

of a country I made

coriander,

bone broth, chilli — all of it

under the door now

the copper sky sits

on the tops of the palm trees

like a second sun

she braids the jasmine

into a rope—I watch it

become what it is

the first drop arrives

on the back of my hand like

a question I pass

spider web at dawn:

six points of contact with the world—

I count mine, fewer

dawn trail, wet red earth—

jugaad holds the whole city

in a spider’s thread

between two puddles

an ant drags a jasmine petal

nobody watching
