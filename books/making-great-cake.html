Making Great Cake

Susan Rocklin
360 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
This book began with a flour sack. Not a metaphor for one — an actual sack, cotton and heavy, leaning against a cabinet in an ordinary kitchen on an ordinary Tuesday. I had been standing in front of it for a long time before I understood that I was not waiting to bake. I was waiting to pay attention. Making Great Cake is a collection of haiku that follows a single birthday cake from pantry shelf to serving platter, but it is also a book about what happens in the small, unhurried spaces of making something for someone you love — the pause before the first measure, the heat building behind a closed oven door, the held breath of a dark hallway full of people pretending to be quiet. Haiku has always known what baking knows: that precision and patience are forms of devotion, that the most important things happen in the narrowest windows of time, and that beauty is often most fully itself in the moment just before it changes. A cake is made to be eaten. A candle is lit to be blown out. These poems try to stay inside those moments — not to preserve them, exactly, but to be honest about how much they hold. What we make for one another, and the care we take in the making, is its own kind of language. This book is an attempt to read it slowly.
