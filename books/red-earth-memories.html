Red Earth Memories

Casey Walker
149 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit here in my Tokyo apartment, surrounded by the hum of neon lights and the soft glow of screens, my mind wanders back to the red earth of Australia where I grew up. The rust-red soil, the wide open skies, and the ancient landscapes that whispered secrets to me as a child – these memories are still so vivid, so alive. In this collection, Red Earth Memories, I've sought to distill those moments into haiku, tiny snapshots of a life lived in various guises. From the golden light of Outback dawns to the fleeting beauty of Ephemeral Seasons, each section is a thread woven into the tapestry of my experiences. As I look back on my journey from rural Australia to urban Japan, I'm struck by the ways in which place and culture have shaped me. These haiku are not just poems – they're doorways to memories, portals to moments that still resonate within me. In these quiet, contemplative lines, I've tried to capture the essence of a life lived between continents, cultures, and seasons. As you read through Red Earth Memories, I hope you'll feel the warmth of my Australian childhood, the curiosity of my British haiku roots, and the vibrant uncertainty of Tokyo's neon haze.
