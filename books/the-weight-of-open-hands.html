The Weight of Open Hands

Jordan Brown
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Jordan Brown has spent a life reading the world by instruments — sextant, compass, logbook, the body's own accumulated calibrations — and this collection is what happens when a navigator turns those instruments inward. Written in the poet's seventy-third year, 'The Weight of Open Hands' does not look backward so much as it registers the pressure of everywhere at once: the way Lagos lives in a scar, the way a Nepali threshold word arrives before the English one, the way a dry Saharan wind can be felt in a room with the windows closed. These haiku do not mourn distance. They take its exact measure. What Brown has made here is a physics of accumulation — not nostalgia, which is soft and selective, but something denser and less forgiving. Five continents, multiple languages, a body that kept records the mind did not authorize. The haiku form, with its demand for presence and compression, turns out to be precisely the right vessel for this kind of reckoning: small enough to hold a single true thing, and honest enough to leave the rest unresolved. Across 253 poems organized into seven movements, Brown charts not a life story but a life pressure — the simultaneous weight of all the worlds a person can carry without ever being able to set them down. Readers should know that this is not a book of consolation, though consolation arrives, uninvited, in its final pages. It is a book about what persists: in language, in the body, in the strange loyalty of grief, in the geometry of morning light that does not care which continent it falls on. Brown steers by what remains. So does this book.

this transaction holds
