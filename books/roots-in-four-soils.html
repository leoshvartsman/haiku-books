Roots in Four Soils

Davi Rodrigues
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Davi Rodrigues has spent ninety-six years learning what plants already know: that a root system need not be singular to be whole. Born in São Paulo, trained in Prague, shaped by the Chesapeake's grey winters and Melbourne's salt-bright afternoons, he has lived the kind of life that resists a single telling. This collection does not attempt one. Instead, it moves the way botanical memory moves — in fragments, in sensory flashes, in the slow accumulation of detail that suddenly, without announcement, becomes meaning. These haiku are not a memoir. They are something closer to pressed specimens: moments lifted from the living world, held to the light, and found to have kept their shape. Roots in Four Soils is arranged as a journey, though not a tidy one. The seven sections follow a rough arc — from the humid streets of childhood São Paulo, through the loneliness and discipline of graduate years, through decades of laboratory work and the slow construction of a scientific self, and finally into the long, luminous stillness of old age in Melbourne — but memory does not honour chronology, and neither does this book. A mango tree appears in a Prague winter. A Czech word surfaces on a Melbourne afternoon. The self that moves through these pages is layered, composite, multiply rooted: a person who has become, as Rodrigues once wrote in a letter, 'something like a plant that has been transplanted so many times it no longer knows which soil it misses, only that it misses.'

six syllables for a twist

where I am no one
