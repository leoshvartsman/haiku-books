Border of the Ordinary

Riley Roy
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A life is not lived in one place, and neither is a self. Border of the Ordinary tracks a single life across four cities and several decades, attending to the small, persistent evidence of how we are shaped by weather, by language, by the names we carry and the names we choose. These haiku do not argue for any grand theory of identity or migration. They notice, instead, the coat still on the hook, the pen still warm, the lid beginning to knock on the pot. They trust that the particular contains the universal, and that the body keeps more accurate records than memory does. Riley Roy came of age in Montreal, found a strange and beautiful displacement in Tennessee, spent a decade being made and unmade by Austin, and arrived at last in Mexico City, where the rain arrives every afternoon like a kept promise. Each place left residue. Each language — French, English, Spanish, the half-language of belonging — left a different kind of mark. This collection is the sediment of that accumulation: not a memoir, not a map, but something closer to the rings inside a tree, visible only in cross-section. Haiku has always been a form preoccupied with the present moment, with the precise weight of right now. It seemed the right vessel for a book about a life that had to learn, again and again, how to be present in a place that did not yet feel like home. The seventeen syllables do not resolve anything. They hold it open. That openness — patient, a little tender, still curious — is what Border of the Ordinary asks of the reader, and what it asks of itself.
