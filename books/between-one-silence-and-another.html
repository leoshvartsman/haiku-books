Between One Silence and Another

Ash Green
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Ash Green has lived in three countries and speaks, with varying degrees of faithfulness, three languages. This book is not about that. Or rather, it is about what accumulates in the silences between such facts — the particular quality of light through a window you no longer live behind, the word that was once as natural as breathing and is now a small, dry absence in the mouth, the way a self, moved repeatedly across the world, eventually stops insisting on its own continuity and begins, instead, to pay attention. These haiku were written over many years, in kitchens and on trains, on the backs of receipts, in the margins of other people's urgencies. They were not written toward anything. That, perhaps, is why they arrived. Haiku has always known what takes other forms longer to learn: that the world does not require our interpretation to proceed, and that our best chance of catching it is to become, briefly, very quiet. Green's haiku practice — rooted in the Japanese tradition but worn into something unmistakably his own — is an act of sustained, undefended listening. He listens to the gaps in his own memory, to the comedy of a life grown deliberately small, to the weathers that have followed him across hemispheres, to the objects that remain after their reasons have departed. He listens, finally, to what he cannot name, and does not try. The seven sections of this collection move not in a straight line but the way attention moves — associatively, doubling back, arriving somewhere unexpected and recognising it anyway. Readers may enter anywhere. The book does not demand to be finished, only met. What it asks is what all haiku ask: a moment of stillness, and the willingness to notice what stillness finds.
