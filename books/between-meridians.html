Between Meridians

Harper Patel
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of knowledge that cities teach you before you know you are being taught. It arrives through the soles of your feet on hot asphalt, through the particular angle of afternoon light on an unfamiliar street, through the smell of rain on a surface that is not the surface you grew up loving. Harper Patel spent years learning to read cities professionally — their grids, their gradients, their zoning classifications and drainage corridors — and discovered, somewhere in the accumulation of that training, that the city had been reading her back. Between Meridians is the record of that mutual study: a collection of haiku that moves through four cities and six languages, tracing the unofficial infrastructure of belonging that no planning document has ever managed to capture. These poems are not travel poems, exactly, though they travel. They are not immigrant poems, exactly, though they carry the particular weight of someone who has learned to locate herself by triangulation rather than by root. What they are is precise — rigorously, almost professionally attentive to the sensory data of place: the way a tram rail fills with rainwater, the paint-layer archaeology of a colonial wall, the silence a mother sends as a fifth language in a four-language text message. Patel brings the planner's eye for detail into collision with the body's stubborn refusal to be mapped, and the haiku form — compressed, imagistic, quietly explosive — turns out to be exactly the right container for that collision. The seven sections of this collection trace a loose arc from childhood memory through intellectual formation, professional practice, and the slow, necessary surrender of expertise to experience. But the arc is not a straight line, and it does not resolve into arrival. It resolves, instead, into something more honest: the recognition that transit itself can be a dwelling, that the self accumulates like layers of paint on an old wall, and that underneath every city — every planned, zoned, optimized city — the red earth is still doing exactly what it wants.

the ant finds it before I

mark it on the plan

the first sun before the street does—

small, deliberate shine

all six languages
