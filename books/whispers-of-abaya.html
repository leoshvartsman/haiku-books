Whispers Of Abaya

Riley Chen
195 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
In the quiet hours, I find myself drawn to the whispers of my past, like the soft rustle of abaya fabric in the Ethiopian breeze. Memories of a life lived between continents and cultures have begun to take shape as haiku – fleeting glimpses into moments of joy, loss, and beauty. As I reflect on the passage of time, I find myself confronting the impermanence that lies at the heart of our existence. Growing up between Addis Ababa and Oxford, I witnessed the delicate balance of tradition and modernity. Later, in Los Angeles and Denver, I discovered new landscapes and ways of being. Through these haiku, I aim to capture not only the essence of these places but also the subtle shifts that occur as we navigate aging and mortality. In these pages, you will find fragments of a life – morning light in Addis, golden hours in Oxford, laughter in LA, snowfall on Denver's roof. The abaya, once a symbol of my mother's cultural heritage, has become a thread connecting these disparate moments, reminding me that even as our lives unfold, we remain rooted in the memories and stories that precede us.
