Displaced Horizons

Robin Sato
180 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit with pen in hand, memories of far-off places and people begin to unfurl like petals on a worn photograph. The haiku that follow are an attempt to capture the essence of displacement – not just a physical move, but a shift in perspective, a recalibration of what home means. Born in Kenya, raised in multiple countries, I've been a stranger in my own stories, searching for words to articulate the disorientation and beauty of being in-between. The haiku in this collection are fragments of that journey – moments of nostalgia and longing, of belonging and non-belonging. They whisper of Nairobi's city lights at dusk, Stanford's sprawling campuses under gray skies, Cape Town's jagged coastlines, Dublin's fading dawns. Each poem is a snapshot of a life lived in transit, a life woven from multiple threads – African soil, foreign streets, and the dreams that refuse to stay still. As I've written these haiku, I've come to realize that displacement is not just about leaving one place for another, but also about being pulled between worlds, torn between roots and routes. These poems are my own navigation of this terrain, a mapping of the crossroads where identity meets migration, and where the familiar becomes strange, and the strange becomes home.
