Every City Teaches the Rain

Tomo Yamamoto
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A city teaches you things you did not sign up to learn. It teaches you where the light falls at four in the afternoon, which streets smell of frying oil after rain, how long a silence has to last before it becomes loneliness rather than peace. Tomo Yamamoto has lived inside four such educations — Osaka, Charlottesville, Casablanca, Sydney — and this collection is the record of what each city wrote on a body that kept moving. The title is not a metaphor so much as a fact: every city does teach the rain, gives it a particular sound against a particular roof, a particular color under a particular kind of neon or fog or noon sun, until rain itself becomes a language with four dialects, and the self becomes someone who speaks all of them imperfectly and cannot stop.
