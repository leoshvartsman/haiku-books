The Ball Goes Far

Frank Ruth
357 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
A baseball leaves the bat in less than a second. What happens before and after that second is the subject of this book. The Ball Goes Far is not a book about baseball the way a box score is about baseball — it is a book about the physics of attention, about what the body knows before the mind catches up, about the strange democracy of a stadium where everyone watches the same thing and sees something different. Johnny Ruth has spent years finding the haiku hiding inside the game, and what he has found is not nostalgia or myth but sensation: the specific weight of a pine-tar handle, the way a crowd rises before it knows why, the sound a ball makes when it is hit exactly right. The collection follows a single trajectory — one swing, one ball, one arc from stillness to landing — and yet each poem stands alone, a complete world in three lines. Ruth borrows the haiku's traditional insistence on the present moment and finds in baseball a sport that is almost entirely made of present moments, each pitch a small eternity of waiting, each swing unrepeatable. The seven sections move like the ball itself: gathering, releasing, climbing, hanging, falling, returning, and finally going quiet. Readers who love baseball will find the game rendered with precision and care. Readers who have never held a bat will find something else — a meditation on force and grace, on the way a single instant can carry meaning long after it has passed. These 387 haiku were written over eleven seasons. They are arranged not by year or by team or by memory, but by the shape of a moment in flight.

the center fielder

where someone stood and forgot—

nobody comes back
