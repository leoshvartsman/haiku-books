Between Altitudes

Robin Novak
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Geography is not just about maps—it lives in the body, in the way altitude changes your breathing, in how your feet remember the particular slant of childhood streets. These haiku trace a life lived between elevations, from the thin air of Andean mornings to the salt-heavy breath of coastal middle age. They are fragments of a journey that spans continents and decades, capturing the small moments that accumulate into a life: frost melting on schoolgirl palms, monsoon rain blurring ledger numbers, the weight of a new surname on official documents. This collection maps the territories we carry within us long after we've left them behind—the muscle memory of work, the accent that surfaces in dreams, the way certain weather brings back entire seasons of being someone else, somewhere else. Between these altitudes, between the mountain-born child and the coast-dwelling adult, lies the country of becoming: uncertain, immersive, transformative, and ultimately, grateful for every elevation along the way.

Sample Haiku

School shoes click rhythms

across ancient cobblestones

worn smooth by centuries

Notebook margins full

of firefly sketches and doubt—

both glow, then vanish

Temple steps gleam wet

with morning rain—I climb

toward evening prayers

Calloused palm meets palm

in the handshake that says yes

we understand this

Trade winds tangle flags

outside the consulate where

I first learned leaving

Wedding ring catches

morning light through kitchen blinds

casting tiny rainbows
