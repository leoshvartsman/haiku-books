Migrating Light

Robin Andersson
160 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit with these haiku, I'm reminded of the moments when language and culture blurred for me – a moment's pause between worlds. The light that filters through my window now is the same one that danced on Istanbul's streets decades ago, or shone bright over the spring blooms in Charlottesville. In this collection, Migrating Light, I've tried to capture those fleeting impressions of a life in transit. Each section is a chapter from my own story, intertwined with the ones I've witnessed and loved along the way. The scenes shift – Tel Aviv's nights, Busan's rainy days, an ephemeral landscape that dissolves as soon as I try to grasp it. These haiku are fragments of memory, imperfect and raw, but perhaps in their imperfection lies a truth about our shared human experience. They whisper secrets of love, loss, and longing – the whispers that linger long after the light has faded.
