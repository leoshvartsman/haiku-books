Between Ignition and Arrival

Arjun Desai
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
These haiku were written by a body that has lived in four cities and learned to stop asking which one it belongs to. Arjun Desai came to haiku not through the tradition but through the work — through the specific silence of a garage at six in the morning, through the particular quality of light on a departure board, through the discovery that the shortest form could hold the most weight without collapsing. The poems in this collection are not about travel. They are about calibration: the slow, mostly unconscious process by which a person adjusts their instruments to a new atmosphere and discovers, in the adjusting, something precise about who they are. The book moves through Mumbai, Miami, Banff, and Johannesburg — not as destinations but as interior states, as weather systems the body has passed through and retained. You will not find grief here, or not grief as elegy. What you will find is the mechanic's attention: unhurried, specific, confident that the small thing in the hand is worth examining fully before moving on. Desai is interested in what the body knows before the mind catches up — the way a crowd on a Mumbai local teaches you to breathe differently, the way Highveld dust on a wheel arch asks to be touched rather than wiped away, the way a joke in one language can be already funny before it has finished crossing into another. Read these poems the way you would inventory a parts shelf: slowly, without skipping, trusting that everything here has a use. The haiku form suits this temperament exactly. It does not explain. It does not arrive at meaning so much as hold the moment still long enough for meaning to become unnecessary. Between ignition and arrival, there is an interval — a half-second, a held breath, a dashboard holding its dark face. Desai has spent years learning to live there, and these poems are what he found.

Sample Haiku

key in the slot—

the dashboard holds its dark face

one more half-second

Papa's motorcycle

in building shadow, cooling—

tick, tick, tick, tick, tick

Marco's collar bone—

I can see it through the shirt,

the whole back glowing

grease under the nail

of my index finger—

the stanza ends here

*je ne comprends pas* —

I say this perfectly, though,

the not-understanding

the joke half-formed still

I'm already laughing—

translation can wait
