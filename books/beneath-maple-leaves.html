Beneath Maple Leaves

Jamie Rodriguez
142 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I wander through the seasons, leaves rustle beneath my feet, and words begin to take shape. This collection, Beneath Maple Leaves, is a gathering of moments – fleeting, yet indelible. In its pages, I've tried to capture the way nature seeps into our lives: in the morning's golden light, in the river's soothing flow, under star-studded New Zealand skies. These haikus are fragments of my own journey, born from strolls through Maine's forests, stays in rural Japan, and reflections on the intersection of human experience and the natural world. In them, I've sought to reveal the intricate web that binds us to our surroundings – a web of threads we weave with every breath. Here, you'll find whispers of morning dew-kissed grasses, the scent of cherry blossoms in spring, and the quiet resilience of Hiroshima's seasons. These poems are invitations to slow down, listen, and breathe with me beneath the maple leaves that rustle through our lives – reminding us that we are a part of something far greater than ourselves.
