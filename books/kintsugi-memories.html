Kintsugi Memories

Sam Brown
181 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit with these haikus, fragments of my life begin to resurface like whispers from a forgotten past. Memories of Sydney's balmy summers, where I first discovered the beauty of imperfection in the worn stones of the Opera House, entwine with Tokyo's neon-lit nights, where the city's frenetic energy became my own heartbeat. In this collection, Kintsugi Memories, I've sought to reassemble the shards of experience that have shaped me. The art of kintsugi – repairing broken pottery with gold – has been a metaphor for my own journey, one marked by breaks and mends, imperfections and beauty. As I reflect on the places that have become home to me – Sydney, Tokyo, Miami, Chicago, and others in between – I've come to see that it's not the smooth surfaces we idealize, but the fractures and the repair work that reveal our truest selves. These haikus are an attempt to capture the essence of those moments: the thrill of discovery, the pain of loss, and the quiet joy of growth. In their simplicity lies a world of complexity – the delicate dance between cultures, languages, and landscapes.
