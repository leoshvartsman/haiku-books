Dust Before the Rain

Sam Sato
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of weather that has nothing to do with the sky. Sam Sato has lived inside it for decades — the interior climate of a person shaped by four cities, four languages, and the particular loneliness of never being entirely from anywhere. Dust Before the Rain is the record of that life, assembled not as memoir but as sensation: the smell of banyan bark after rain, the red ink pooling at a manuscript's margin, the silence before a muezzin's call fills a Dubai morning. These haiku do not explain displacement. They inhabit it. Written across years and continents — Taipei, Dhaka, Dubai, and the unnamed rooms where translation happens at two in the morning — this collection moves through nostalgia, disorientation, surrender, and finally a hard-won stillness that refuses to be called resolution. Sato works in the haiku tradition's oldest faith: that a single image, held long enough, opens into something larger than itself. A cracked sidewalk square. A ceiling fan's slow argument with heat. The interpreter's pause. Each poem is a room the reader is invited to stand inside, briefly, and feel the temperature of another person's interior world. The title comes from that suspended moment before a monsoon breaks — when the earth releases its oldest smell and the air holds everything at once: anticipation, memory, the body's animal readiness. It is, Sato suggests, the condition of a life lived between languages and latitudes. Not lost. Not arrived. But exquisitely, uncomfortably, beautifully present in the waiting.
