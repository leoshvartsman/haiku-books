The Longitude of Staying

Riley García
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Riley García has spent a lifetime in the company of other people's words — ordering them, shelving them, pressing them into strangers' hands with the quiet authority of someone who knows what a person needs before they do. "The Longitude of Staying" is what happens when that lifetime finally turns inward. These 250 haiku move across eight decades and four cities, tracing a single consciousness as it learns, loses, protests, ages, and — with something that is neither resignation nor triumph but closer to clear-eyed tenderness — settles. The title comes from a question García says she stopped being able to answer in her sixties: not where she was from, but what it had cost her to remain anywhere at all. The book is organized not strictly by chronology but by weather — the weather of a life, its cold openings and dry middles and the long amber light of its late afternoons. Haiku here are not decorative. They are instruments of precision, chosen because the form refuses sentimentality the way a good bookseller refuses a bad return: firmly, without cruelty. What accumulates across these seven sections is a portrait of how place becomes politics, how politics become the body, and how the body, in the end, becomes the only home that was ever truly portable. Readers who have loved a city, lost a country, or spent years in service to other people's stories will find something here that feels, uncomfortably and gratefully, like being known.
