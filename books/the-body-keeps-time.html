The Body Keeps Time

Jordan Rodriguez
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of knowing that lives below the neck. It is in the way a musician's left hand finds a chord in the dark, in the way the feet recognize a particular street before the eyes do, in the way a freight train felt through gravel in childhood becomes, decades later, the measure by which all vibration is understood. Jordan Rodriguez has spent a life moving between cities, between languages, between the person who plays and the person who listens — and this collection is the record of what the body kept when the mind was busy forgetting. The haiku gathered here follow a life from its earliest tuning — the hum of a screen door, a field of grasshoppers finding pitch together — through the discipline of craft, the sensory education of Chicago, the restless suspension of departure, and finally into the slow astonishment of a new country, a second language, a different quality of light on stone. These are not poems about music so much as poems that move the way music moves: by interval, by silence, by the thing that happens between one note and the next. Rodriguez trusts the reader to hear what is not written. The seven sections of this book are less a chronology than a progression in the harmonic sense — each one a new key that contains and transforms what came before. By the final poems, the guitar stands in the corner unasked, the thunder answers back across thirty years of road, and something that might be called resolution settles in, not as an ending but as a chord that has finally decided to hold. The body, it turns out, has been keeping time all along.
