Cities Beneath the Skin

Sol Díaz
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a geography written beneath our skin—not of borders or boundaries, but of morning light slanting through unfamiliar windows, of street sounds that become lullabies, of the particular way rain falls in each place we've called home. This collection traces the invisible maps we carry, the cities that continue to breathe within us long after we've locked their doors for the last time. Through seven movements across four cities, these haiku explore how place and memory interweave, how the concrete particulars of one landscape echo in another, and how the body holds what the mind cannot always name. From Mediterranean stone to Atlantic storms, from mountain air thin with altitude to bamboo groves thick with mist, each poem is a small archaeology of belonging—an attempt to understand not just where we've been, but how those places continue to shape the contours of who we become.

Morning light cascades

down terracotta slopes—

pigeons count the tiles

Workshop door slams shut—

outside, beach plover runs

from wave's reaching fingers

Platform crowds surge forward

briefcases and shopping bags

fleeing toward fresh air

Turbulence rattles

ice cubes in plastic cups while

clouds swallow the wings

Koi swim through their own

reflections, teaching water

the meaning of depth

Four shadows cross here—

Barcelona, Provincetown,

Mexico, Kyoto
