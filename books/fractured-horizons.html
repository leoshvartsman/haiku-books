Fractured Horizons

Jamie Johnson
132 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit at my desk, surrounded by the faint scent of old books and the distant hum of city traffic, I'm reminded that poetry is often born from fragments – whispers in the dark, echoes of forgotten conversations, glimpses of lives lived on the margins. My own life has been a tapestry of contrasts: Edinburgh's grey skies and New York's concrete jungle, Taipei's neon-lit alleys and Nairobi's vibrant markets. It's this sense of dislocation that fuels my writing – the feeling of being suspended between worlds. Fractured Horizons is more than just a collection of haikus; it's an attempt to weave together the disparate threads of our shared human experience. In these poems, I've tried to capture the beauty in the brokenness – the way light filters through cracked windows, the sound of distant laughter carried on the wind, the feel of worn earth beneath bare feet. It's a journey that's taken me from city streets to rural landscapes, and back again. As you read through these haikus, I hope you'll catch glimpses of yourself – in the lines of a forgotten poem scrawled on a subway wall, in the echoes of a childhood spent playing in the shadow of a factory, or in the faces that pass by like strangers on the street. My own stories are here, intertwined with those of others who've lived and loved and lost their way in this vast, messy world.
