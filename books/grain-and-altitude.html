Sample Haiku

fog lifts off Limmat

leaving one grey heron still

where the water was

linden branch bends low

under nothing visible—

just the weight of cold

sawdust on my coat

warmer than the wool beneath

the workshop holds heat

carpenter’s pencil,

flat edge on pine — the line stays

truer than I planned

sawdust on my boots

at dusk, pale gold, still settling

long after I stop

scaffold sways a half

inch in November wind — I

grip, then I let go

my manuscript bleeds

red into the margin where

I meant certainty

fluorescent hum drops

a half-step—everyone shifts

papers, says nothing

geese lift together

from the Charles without warning—

the water forgets

she laughs at my accent—

not unkindly—I laugh too,

madera, Holz, wood

the Zoom freezes on

my father’s mouth mid-sentence—

I fill in the word

a dream in pure French,

a language I rarely use—

I wake embarrassed

a Santiago dawn:

first the buses, then the light,

then the mountains, last

the dog follows me

halfway up, then turns back—

he knows where I live

jacaranda on

the windshield of a parked truck—

no one will notice

pigeons on the ledge

of the colonial church —

they do not pray, stay

I check the joint with

my thumbnail — in the market,

I check the peach the same

a broom leaned on a

stall post, mid-morning. No one

sweeping, no one gone.
