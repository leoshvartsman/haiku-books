The Distance Between Rains

Ying Hu
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Weather is not backdrop. In Ying Hu's hands, it is the medium through which a life is felt and measured — the monsoon of childhood, the Pacific fog that asks nothing of you, the Dublin drizzle that requires a different kind of patience, the London grey that eventually becomes, against all expectation, your own. These two hundred and fifty haiku trace a passage across meridians and decades, mapping not the grand events of a life but its atmospheric texture: the quality of light on a particular morning, the way a carousel of black cases can produce a specific species of loneliness, the slow education of hands in foreign soil. This is a book about displacement, but it refuses displacement's usual grief. What Hu discovers, poem by poem, is that each place does not replace the last but deposits something inside you — a particular angle of light, a smell, a way that water moves. The self that emerges is not fragmented but layered, a kind of geological record. The haiku form, with its discipline of attention and its trust in the concrete image, turns out to be the perfect instrument for this kind of archaeology: it insists on the present moment even when the present moment is saturated with elsewhere. The collection moves roughly chronologically, from a harbour childhood through crossing and arrival and the long work of rooting, toward a meridian reckoning and finally a hard-won stillness. But it is not a linear journey so much as a spiral one — water reappears, windows recur, the same gestures of attention are made in different latitudes. By the end, the distance between rains is not emptiness. It is where the reader, like the poet, has been learning to live.
