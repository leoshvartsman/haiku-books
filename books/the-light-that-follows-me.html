The Light That Follows Me

Sam Martin
246 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Sam Martin was born in Quito under a light so particular — equatorial, vertical, arriving each morning without apology — that every latitude he has lived in since has been measured against it. This collection follows him across eight decades and four cities, from the volcanic highlands of Ecuador to the limestone plateaus of Tennessee, from the steel and ginkgo canyons of Chicago to the salt-scrubbed stones of Barcelona. It is not a travel book. It is something closer to a reckoning: the slow discovery that urban nature does not change its essential grammar whether spoken in Spanish, English, or Catalan — only its accent, only the angle of the light. Haiku, with its insistence on the present moment and its distrust of abstraction, proved the natural form for this kind of looking. Martin came to the form late, after a long career in advertising, and that timing matters. These are not the poems of someone who has always known how to be still. They are the poems of someone who learned stillness the hard way, who spent decades writing headlines for things he did not believe in and arrived at old age hungry for a single true sentence. What he found, in the pigeons and iron grates and cold sand and sparrows, was that the world had been offering him true sentences all along. The seven sections of this book move roughly in chronological order, but time in haiku is never quite linear. The child in Quito and the old man on the Barcelona breakwater are always, in some sense, looking at the same light. Readers may find themselves moving backward as often as forward, recognizing in the final poems the same quality of attention — wide-eyed, undefended — that opens the first. That, perhaps, is the book's quiet argument: that wonder is not something we grow out of, only something we have to find our way back to, city by city, decade by decade, one small careful observation at a time.
