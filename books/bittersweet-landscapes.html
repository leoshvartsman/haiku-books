Bittersweet Landscapes

Andrea Marino
149 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
As I sit at my desk, surrounded by scraps of paper and half-empty tea cups, I'm transported back to the countless places that have shaped me. The cypress-lined streets of Florence, where as a child I'd chase pigeons through the Palazzo Pitti gardens. The dusty savannahs of Nairobi, where the roar of lions pierced the night air. And the rolling hills of rural Ireland, where my grandmother's stories of revolution and love would unfold like a gentle mist. These places have become threads in the tapestry of my life, woven together with memories of laughter and tears, of protests and quiet moments of reflection. In this collection, I've tried to capture the essence of each landscape, allowing its spirit to bleed into the haiku that have been my solace, my anchor, and my window onto the world. In these pages, you'll find the bittersweet beauty of a place caught between past and present, where politics and nature collide. You'll see fragments of love letters scrawled on napkins in Florence's cafes, hear the distant rumble of Nairobi's traffic, feel the wind rustle through Irish barley fields. These are not just landscapes – they're thresholds to stories that can only be whispered, between heartbeats.
