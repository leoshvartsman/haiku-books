Borrowed Light

Morgan Andersson
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a kind of light that belongs to no single place — the light that falls through a tram window, or across a stone floor in a foreign city, or onto a bowl of broth at a stall that will be folded away by morning. Morgan Andersson has spent a life moving through such light, and this collection is an attempt to hold it still long enough to see by. Zurich, Kyoto, Florence, Busan: four cities that do not explain each other, stitched together not by narrative but by the body that passed through them — the same hands that learned to count tiles, to press an ink stick, to carry a bucket of plaster, to sort anchovies by length. 'Borrowed Light' does not grieve its own displacement. It does not romanticize the migrant's eye or treat foreignness as a permanent credential. What it does instead is quieter and more difficult: it insists on presence. On the weight of a tram on wet tracks. On the smell of kiln smoke at five in the morning. On the particular blue of a Korean winter sky that cannot be translated but can, perhaps, be pointed at. The haiku form — spare, bodily, committed to the instant — turns out to be the ideal vessel for a life lived in transit, because it makes no room for the explanations that transit so often demands. The collection moves in rough chronology, from a Swiss childhood through apprenticeships in Japan and Italy, through the suspended non-time of airports and arrivals, into a life slowly, stubbornly put down in Busan. But the deeper movement is not through time. It is through the body's own archive — the memory stored in a thumb finding a worn groove in stone, in paint-stiffened bristles holding the shape of yesterday's work. That archive is the only home these poems claim, and by the final section, it turns out to be enough.
