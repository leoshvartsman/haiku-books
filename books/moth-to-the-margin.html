Moth to the Margin

Saoirse Kelly
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
There is a particular quality of attention that takes decades to develop — not the sharp focus of someone looking for something, but the peripheral alertness of someone who has stopped insisting on where to look. Saoirse Kelly has been keeping that kind of watch for twenty-eight years, across cities and languages and the ordinary furniture of a life lived with unusual wakefulness. What she has found, consistently, is that meaning declines to stand in the centre of things. It hovers at the edge of the frame. It arrives in the wrong moment, through the wrong door, wearing the smell of a soap that hasn't been manufactured since 1985. 'Moth to the Margin' is organised not as a journey with a destination but as a sustained act of peripheral vision — seven sections that circle the same quiet discovery from different angles. The body knows things the mind has filed away. Doorways hold the geometry of choices. The people no one writes odes to are frequently the ones keeping the lights on. Language breaks down precisely when it is most needed, and objects outlast every intention we had for them. At three in the morning, the mathematics of a life become briefly, brutally clear. And somewhere in all of this — in the hem of it, the afterglow — something that might be called learning, or joy, or simply the willingness to keep noticing, refuses to stop. These 250 haiku do not ask to be read quickly. They are written by someone who has learned that the almost-missed moment will not wait, but can, sometimes, be caught — not by lunging at it but by having been, quietly, already there.
