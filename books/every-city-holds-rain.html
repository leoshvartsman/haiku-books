Every City Holds Rain

Ming Xu
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Every city holds rain, but no two cities hold it the same way. Shanghai rain arrives like a memory you didn't know you had — warm, insistent, smelling of the river. New York rain is argument. Chicago rain is fact. Berlin rain falls as though it has been waiting for you specifically, as though the sky kept your address. Ming Xu has lived inside all of these weathers, and this collection is what the body retains when the mind has moved on: the particular slant of light through a particular window, the sound a tram makes turning a corner you are only beginning to know, the way a name sits differently in the mouth depending on which country is listening. These 250 haiku move through four cities and across four decades of a life, tracing the arc from inheritance to invention — from a childhood in Shanghai half-dissolved by distance, through the restless reinventions of New York, the quiet devotion of Chicago years spent among books, the suspended unreality of a pandemic, the grief and freedom of leaving again, and finally the cautious wonder of arriving in Berlin as a middle-aged stranger. The collection asks, with patience and without sentimentality, what we carry when we leave and what we become when we stay — and whether those two questions ever stop being the same question. Haiku, a form built on the instant, turns out to be the right vessel for a life lived at the border of languages. Each poem is a moment of contact: between self and weather, between name and identity, between the person who left and the person who arrived. Xu does not resolve these tensions so much as learn to hold them — the way you hold a book you have carried across an ocean, feeling its weight as both burden and proof that you were somewhere, that you read, that you stayed long enough to know the dust on the top shelf without looking up.
