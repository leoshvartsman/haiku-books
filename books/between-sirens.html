Between Sirens

Morgan Andersson
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Between the urgent wail of sirens lies a world of profound stillness—moments when breath catches, when time suspends, when the ordinary reveals itself as sacred. This collection traces a geography of becoming: from the monsoon-soaked streets of childhood Dhaka to the ancient cobblestones of Rome, connected by the thread of a paramedic's calling to witness life at its most raw and tender. These haiku are born from the spaces between emergency and peace, migration and belonging, the weight of others' pain and the lightness of morning espresso. They map a journey not just across continents, but across the landscapes of identity—where a child's wonder at jackfruit thunder becomes an adult's reverence for basil whispering to Roman light. In the tradition of haiku's seasonal awareness, this collection honors the seasons of a life: the urgent spring of discovery, the full summer of service, the autumn of searching, and the winter wisdom of finally coming home to oneself.

Sample Haiku

Mother's cotton sari

spreads across the rooftop line—

blue sail catching stars

Fog clings to ridges—

my first English poem bleeds

through borrowed grammar

Radio silence

after we call time of death—

even static mourns

Security guard nods

as I badge out at seven—

witnesses to dawn

Espresso steam curls

toward terracotta heaven—

Rome wakes in my cup

golden hour stretches

across my kitchen windowsill

thyme shadows lengthen
