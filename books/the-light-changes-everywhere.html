The Light Changes Everywhere

Dakota Young
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
These poems were not planned. They arrived the way most true things do — sideways, in the margins, during the minutes before a deposition began or after a trail disappeared into fog. Dakota Young spent years as a lawyer learning to read language for what it concealed, for the load-bearing clause buried in the subordinate clause, for the word chosen precisely because it gave nothing away. The haiku in this collection are the opposite of that training and, in some ways, its completion: small structures that hold open rather than close down, that ask the reader to stand still long enough for the world to move.
