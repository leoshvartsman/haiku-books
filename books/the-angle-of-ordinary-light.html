The Angle of Ordinary Light

Xia Zhao

Download PDF Download EPUB
Xia Zhao has lived in four countries and spoken four languages, and this book is not about any of them. It is about the hours that do not make it into stories: the particular resistance of a drawer in a rented flat, the way afternoon light behaves in Bergen versus Shanghai, the muscle memory of hands that have learned to work in more than one world. At 88, she is not interested in the grand arc of a life. She is interested in what remains when the arc is set aside — texture, temperature, the slow grammar of the unremarkable. Haiku, with its insistence on the present moment and its suspicion of explanation, turns out to be the right form for this kind of attention. These 253 poems do not tell you what four countries feel like from the inside. They show you a pot lid, a frost-covered window, a green spike of ginger on a January sill, a hearing aid resting beside a water glass. They trust that the small thing, looked at long enough, opens into something larger without ever needing to announce it. The collection moves loosely through territories of experience rather than strict chronology — the body's knowledge, the interior life of enclosed spaces, the rituals of cooking, the bureaucratic and emotional weight of borders, the persistence of growing things, the strangeness of other people's seasons, and finally the new negotiations of a body at 88. Readers are invited to move through these sections as Xia Zhao moves through a room: without hurry, with full attention to what the light is doing.
