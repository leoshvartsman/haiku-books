Rain On The City

Morgan Durand
125 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
I remember the way rain lapped against the Seine's stone banks on gray mornings in Paris. The world seemed to slow, and I was lost in the city's symphony of sounds: dripping water, rustling leaves, and distant laughter. It was there that I began to collect fragments of life – moments so ordinary they felt almost magical. As I traveled, this vision persisted: urban landscapes holding secrets, rhythms pulsating beneath the surface. Rio's Carnival dreams swirled with vibrant colors, Johannesburg nights whispered tales of resilience, and university whispers shared the language of strangers. I found myself drawn to these ephemeral scenes, where beauty was woven from daily lives. In Rain on the City, I've attempted to distill these fragments into haiku – short glimpses at life's intricate tapestry.
