Wherever the Jasmine Follows

Saanvi Nair
250 haiku
Download PDF Download EPUB
Saanvi Nair has lived in four places that do not resemble one another. Bangalore gave her camphor smoke and steel cups and the particular quality of a morning before the ceiling fan begins. Sydney gave her salt light and fluorescent offices and the strange comedy of being new. Scotland gave her heather and haar and a silence that asked nothing of her and expected nothing back. Florence gave her ochre and blood oranges and the recurring grace of beginning again. This collection does not argue that these places add up to a self. It argues something quieter and more difficult: that the self is the adding up, the daily act of tending, the jasmine carried in a paper bag across every border. Haiku has always understood that the largest things arrive in the smallest apertures — a cup sweating in morning heat, a gannet there and then not there, a door-sound at the end of a voice note. Nair works in this tradition with both fidelity and freedom, using the form's compression not to simplify experience but to honour its actual texture: the way belonging reveals itself not in grand arrival but in increments, in the wrong lentils going soft into something good, in a visa photo aging into someone you are still becoming. These poems do not mourn the rootless life or romanticise it. They live inside it, eyes open, breathing steadily. "Wherever the Jasmine Follows" moves chronologically through geography but not straightforwardly through time — memory doubles back, landscapes echo one another, the woman in Florence still carries the woman in Bangalore inside her like a second climate. Readers may find themselves, as Nair does, discovering that home is less a coordinate than a practice: something revised, tended, occasionally dropped and picked back up, never quite finished, always — stubbornly, essentially — in progress.
