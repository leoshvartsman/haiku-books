Dakota Young

2 collections · Hong Kong, China

Dakota Young's work moves between two kinds of attention: the Angeleno eye learning a new city, and the body that keeps walking anyway — through Hong Kong summers, along trails that disappear into fog, into rooftop gardens pressed between glass and heat. Concrete jungle. Unexpected green. A transplant learning to read a landscape sideways.

The work asks what it means to find nature in a city that offers only fragments of it — and whether those fragments are enough. The question stays open, somewhere between the sweltering air and the light that keeps changing, everywhere, all at once.
