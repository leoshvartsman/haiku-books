Frank Ruth

1 collection

Frank Ruth's work lives in the geometry of a single moment: the crack of a bat, the arc of a ball climbing past the warning track, the physics of how far a thing can travel when struck correctly. *The Ball Goes Far* is not a scoreboard. It is a book about the second before and the long silence after — attention measured in hang time and exit velocity.

The work asks what it means to truly watch something leave. Not the box score, not the stat line — the actual departure. It holds that question in the air, the way a well-hit ball does, before anything lands.
