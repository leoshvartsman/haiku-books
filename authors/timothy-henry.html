Timothy Henry

1 collection

Timothy Henry's work begins where the simple things refuse to stay simple. A ball. A field of grass or mud or cracked summer earth. Rules that fit on a few pages, learned in an afternoon. From these coordinates — the clean edge of a rulebook, the unpredictable surface underfoot — he writes with the kind of attention that follows a thing past where it seems to end.

The work asks what happens when something plain keeps reaching. Where the boundary of a game becomes a question about games themselves, about rules and what they cannot hold. No resolution offered. Only the next pass, the next patch of ground, and the question still moving.
