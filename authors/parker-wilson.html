Parker Wilson

2 collections

Parker Wilson's work moves between Brisbane heat and Stockholm cold, between jacaranda stain and snowfall, between the ancient medina and the modern street. Bare feet on hot bitumen. Frost-printed grass. The body as the first instrument of place, reading the world through skin and sole before any word is written.

The work asks what happens when a single life spans contradictory coordinates — sun-drenched and frost-bitten, rooted and in transit. No resolution is offered. Only the specific hour, the specific surface underfoot, held long enough to become something.
