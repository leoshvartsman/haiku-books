Parker Lewis

1 collection · Kolkata, India

Parker Lewis writes from the gap between pulse and breath — from Austin's sun-baked streets to Kolkata's monsoon-washed wards, with three other countries mapped in between. A traveling nurse. A decade of threshold moments. The stethoscope as compass, the body as territory.

The work asks what it means to tend — to others, to oneself, to the places that leave their mark. Not a clean answer anywhere in it. Only the daily observations, held close, tracing the intimate cartography of care across distances that don't shrink with time.
