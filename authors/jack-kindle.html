Jack Kindle

1 collection

Jack Kindle's work begins in the quiet accumulation of ordinary objects: the dried ring a mug leaves on a windowsill, the habit of buying two of something when you only need one. A room that is both empty and full at once. These are the coordinates — not places on a map, but conditions of the interior, where loneliness settles without announcing itself.

The work asks how two people find each other across that particular silence — and whether connection, when it arrives, can reach something longer than a single life. No resolution is offered. Only the space between the mug and the windowsill, held open.
