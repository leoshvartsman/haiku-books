Skyler Anderson

1 collection · Dhaka, Bangladesh

Skyler Anderson's work moves between a granite-cold coast in Maine and the streets of Dhaka, tracing what happens when a voice must learn to shape itself around foreign syllables. Pine sap. Unfamiliar teacups. The weight of things carried across water. Rivers that have no name until someone far from home gives them one.

The work asks what survives a crossing — what accumulates in the body, what gets left on the shore. It finds no resolution. Only the moments themselves, held together: the tremor of hands, the sound of a self still learning where it belongs.
