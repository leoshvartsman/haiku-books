Cameron Brown

2 collections

Cameron Brown's work begins in particular places: Jakarta sidewalks in afternoon rain, the quiet of a morning before the steam rises. Childhood memory as a starting point. The haiku's pause. A life measured in small observances — a raindrop's path, a shift in seasonal light.

The work asks what gets lost in the ordinary moment if no one stops to look — and what gets recovered when someone does. Jakarta and its rain. Two hundred fifty small windows onto the world. The question stays open, one haiku at a time.
