Susan Rocklin

1 collection

Susan Rocklin's work begins with a flour sack — cotton, heavy, leaning against a cabinet on an ordinary Tuesday. From that ordinary kitchen, something opens. One book. A birthday. The materials of celebration examined with patience: flour, sugar, the specific weight of a sack you stand in front of long enough to understand.

The work asks what happens in the ordinary moment before understanding arrives — and what a cake, made well, actually is. Not a metaphor. The thing itself. The question stays in the kitchen, unresolved, smelling of butter and time.
