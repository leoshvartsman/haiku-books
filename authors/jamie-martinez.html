Jamie Martinez

3 collections

Jamie Martinez's work moves through the small hours of ordinary weather: morning rain in Miami, first snow in Vermont, a Bangkok afternoon that refuses to cool. The haiku form carries these moments — cherry blossoms, ancient winds, the digital present pressing against tradition. Seasons changing. A life taken in increments.

The work asks what survives when a moment passes — whether a few syllables can hold rain, or heat, or the particular quality of a morning before anyone else is awake. No resolution offered. Only the next small thing, noticed.
