Sacha Michel

2 collections

Sacha Michel moves through seasons the way a river moves through granite — slowly, accumulating. Fallen leaves on a forest floor. The cold coast of Maine. The spaces between stones where silence settles and takes its shape. These haiku gather what most days discard: the fragment, the encounter, the impression that passes before it can be named.

The work turns on a quiet problem — that a river does not know its name until someone else gives it one. That memory and meaning arrive late, after the wandering. These collections hold that gap open: the distance between living a moment and understanding what it was.
