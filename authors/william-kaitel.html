William Kaitel

1 collection

William Kaitel's work begins in rehearsal — in the rooms where gestures are learned, the faces studied until they become familiar, the small repeated acts of passing the salt. Three collections. A precise attention. A life measured in what gets handed down.

The work asks what it means to inherit a performance you didn't choose — the lines absorbed before you knew you were listening. No resolution is offered. Only the writing itself, which holds the question steady.
