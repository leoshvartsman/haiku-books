Upside down Sactown

1 collection

Upside Down Sactown's haikus begin with Yacob — a man with a spoon, a bowl, one shoe tied, a front door he may or may not reach. Sacramento somewhere underneath it all, inverted. The coordinates here are domestic and specific: the small geometry of a morning, the ordinary objects a person moves through without asking to be observed.

The work holds one question open and does not let go of it: why would you do this. Not why write about Yacob, exactly — but why hand that task to a machine. The spoon is still there. The bowl. Yacob did not ask. The haikus exist anyway, which is either the problem or the whole point.
