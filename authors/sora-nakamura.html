Sora Nakamura

2 collections

Sora Nakamura moves through misty Japanese mornings, New Zealand's coastlines, China's ancient streets — finding something to stop at in each one. Fingerprints on glass. The particular angle of dawn over a mountain, over an ocean. Two hundred and fifty haiku and still counting.

The work asks what a fleeting moment owes us, if anything — whether the light creeping over water is enough, or whether noticing it is the thing itself. No resolution. Only the next crossing, the next threshold, the shadows that arrive just before the day does.
