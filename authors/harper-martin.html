Harper Martin

2 collections · Paris, France

Harper Martin's work begins in the body — not the body as metaphor, but the body as archive. The wrist over the kettle. The shoulder's angle. What the mind revises, the body files without consent. Four collections. Portland as base, but the coordinates are interior: trauma as geography, sensation as record, the self as a landscape that keeps its own latitude regardless of what story gets told over it.

The work asks what survives when memory is unreliable but sensation is not — and whether that distinction offers shelter or only a different kind of exposure. The threshold holds. The smoke stays between.
