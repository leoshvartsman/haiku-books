River Smith

2 collections

River Smith's work moves through the in-between: a snowflake in Zurich, a sunset over California, a gray Chicago sky, a fig left on the branch. Haiku built from fragments of seasons and the memories that cling to them. Corridors walked too fast. A table steadied by hand. The hairline fractures in ordinary moments.

The question underneath it all: what lives inside the threshold — the pause before arrival, the thing not quite finished. A snowflake, a fig, a corridor. The work holds these open, turns them slowly, and waits.
