Ming Xu

3 collections

Ming Xu's work moves through steel and stone, through foreign cities and childhood streets, through the space between a stranger's voice and something half-remembered. Haikus gathered from intersecting stories. A tapestry that keeps unraveling at the edges.

The work asks what the self becomes when the familiar and the unfamiliar share the same sky — when urban facades reflect something interior and broken. No resolution. Only the whispered conversations, the labyrinth, the light fading over cities that never quite belonged to anyone.
