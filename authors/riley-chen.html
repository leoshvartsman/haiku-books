Riley Chen

2 collections

Riley Chen's work moves between the Ethiopian breeze and Philadelphia snow, between abaya fabric and storm drain runoff, between haiku and the archaeology of bottle caps in river mud. The coordinates shift — Addis to the Schuylkill, the continent-crossing past to the street dog carrying someone's lost shoe — but the attention stays fixed on what a single moment holds.

The work asks what gets carried and what gets lost when life moves across those distances. The tuk-tuk, the melting snow, the quiet hours — each one a container for something larger than itself. The question stays open in the details.
