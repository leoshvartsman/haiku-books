Ryan Walsh

2 collections

Ryan Walsh's work begins in the quiet hours before dawn — in shadows on walls, in a father's voice carrying distant lands into a childhood bedroom. Four cities. Four languages. Ninety-six years of paying attention. The record of that attention is not kept in sequence but in the way memory actually moves: burning, drifting, dispersing like smoke.

The work asks what remains when the ordinary moment — the shadow, the story overheard, the smell of something burning — is held long enough to mean something. No resolution. Only the next moment, and the attention brought to it.
