Morgan Durand

2 collections

Morgan Durand's work moves through the particular textures of a life lived between cities: the gray mornings of Paris, rain against stone banks, the slow accumulation of doorways and thresholds. Born in the early years of the last century. A life that passed through Johannesburg. Hands that learned and forgot and learned again.

The work asks what can be found in the unhurried moment — the lapping water, the rustling leaf, the distant laughter — when a life refuses to arrange itself into narrative. No clean story is offered. Only the thresholds themselves, held open.
