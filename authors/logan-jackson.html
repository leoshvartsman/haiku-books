Logan Jackson

2 collections

Logan Jackson's work moves between a small apartment in New Orleans and the open water beyond it — jazz drifting through windows, beignets, the slow pull of the Mississippi, Mardi Gras crowds thinning into memory. The everyday surfaces hold everything: a summer afternoon, a scent, a parade rounding a corner and gone.

What stays when the moment passes? The work sits with that question — not the grand journey but the residue of it, the way a room in New Orleans can open, without warning, onto the Rio de la Plata.
