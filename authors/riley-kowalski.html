Riley Kowalski

3 collections · Athens, Greece

Riley Kowalski works in the space between thimbles and Wehrmacht boots, between Anne Frank's chestnut tree and red tulips pushing through sidewalk cracks. The collections move through grandmother's silver, salt stains, marble steps, the turning of seasons — small objects that carry the weight of what came before.

The work asks how ordinary surfaces hold history. A queue of whispering visitors. A worn thumb-groove in metal. The question isn't answered so much as turned over, season by season, the way a thimble catches light differently depending on where you're standing when you look.
