Casey García

2 collections · Reykjavik, Iceland

Casey García's work moves at the pace of a nature walk through Reykjavik — attentive, unhurried, learning the names of things. Latin binomials. Leaf margins. Root systems. Fig split by noon heat. Jasmine through a crack. Cedar holding snow it will not remember. The coordinates shift — slope, limestone wall, plaster dust on sill — but the method stays the same: patient, exacting attention to what grows, what clings, what remains.

The work asks what roots actually remember, and whether the vocabulary of botany is equal to the task. Cedar and cordite. A mother's wrist. A country whose pink mouth will not close. No answer arrives. Only the names, held carefully, in tension with what they cannot contain.
