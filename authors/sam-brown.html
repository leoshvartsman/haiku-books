Sam Brown

3 collections

Sam Brown's work moves between Sydney's worn harbour stones and Tokyo rooftops, through Miami's Art Deco beaches and the hidden corners of cities half-remembered. Kintsugi. Salt. The particular weight of air before rain. A body that learns things before the mind has words for them.

The work asks what accumulates when a life is lived across latitudes — not as loss, not as gain, but as the warmth stone holds long after the sun has moved. No clean accounting. Only the ordinary moment, held up to the light until it shows its fracture lines.
