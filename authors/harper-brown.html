Harper Brown

2 collections

Harper Brown's work moves between coastlines and city streets, between New Zealand's rugged edges and the rhythms of Boston. Red earth underfoot. Laboratory silence at dawn. A constellation of specific places that refuse to resolve into a single home.

The work asks what accumulates in ordinary moments — the light on water, the weight of familiar ground, the hush of a room before the day begins. Not grand crossings, but the small residue of each place on a life. The question stays open.
