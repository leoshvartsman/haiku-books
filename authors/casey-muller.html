Casey Müller

1 collection · Paris, France

Casey Müller's work moves between Stockholm and Paris, through the slow arithmetic of latitudes and years. Seven sections. More than two hundred haiku. A life measured not in calendars but in coordinates — the distance between languages, between bodies, between the self that was and the one that remains.

The work asks what it means to track a life as it narrows — what coordinates hold when the body begins to forget its own longitude. No resolution offered. Only the haiku themselves, which keep the latitude open, one degree at a time.
