Sky Singh

2 collections · Melbourne, Australia

Sky Singh's work moves between hemispheres and seasons, accumulating what the body knows before the mind catches up — the slant of afternoon light that means something different on each continent, the weight of air before rain, the way stone holds warmth. Eight decades. Multiple cities. A life kept between ledgers and the silences ledgers cannot hold. Melbourne, now. The kigo as a kind of reckoning.

The work asks what it means to carry seasonal knowledge across latitudes — when the body's calendar no longer matches the sky overhead. Salt between places. Thresholds that open onto the wrong time of year. The question stays open. Only the light, arriving at its particular angle, insisting on an answer.
