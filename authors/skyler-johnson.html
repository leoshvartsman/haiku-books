Skyler Johnson

2 collections

Skyler Johnson's work moves through San Francisco mornings and a Kathmandu garden tended for nearly two decades. Light that arrives and disappears. Arrangements made not in order but the way a life accumulates — sideways, in the margins of ordinary time.

The work asks what gets held in a fleeting moment — whether the whisper left behind after the light is gone is loss or something else entirely. No clean answer. Only the moments themselves, kept open.
