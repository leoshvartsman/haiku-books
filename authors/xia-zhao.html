Xia Zhao

3 collections

Xia Zhao has moved through Shanghai, the Hudson, Norwegian mountains, and the Ōta River delta — that same delta where the river ran with the dead in August 1945. Eight decades of arriving. Haiku as a travel companion through cities and seasons. The silences between places as a language of their own.

The work asks what remains when impermanence is the only constant — what a syllable can hold, what a river remembers, what survives the crossing from one shore to another. No clean answer. Only the quiet intimacy of the form itself, keeping the question open.
